### Diagnostic Criteria (DSM-5)

Mental disorder requires:
1. **Disturbance** in cognition, emotion regulation, or behavior
2. **Dysfunction** in psychological, biological, or developmental processes
3. **Distress or disability** in social, occupational, or other important activities
4. Not **culturally sanctioned** response
5. Not primarily from **social deviance** or conflict

### Suicide Risk Assessment

**High-Risk Factors**:

```
SAD PERSONS Scale (Risk Factors):

S = Sex (male)
A = Age (<19 or >45, especially elderly)
D = Depression
P = Previous attempt (strongest predictor)
E = Ethanol/substance abuse
R = Rational thinking loss (psychosis)
S = Social support lacking
O = Organized plan (specific, lethal)
N = No spouse (divorced, widowed, single)
S = Sickness (chronic illness, pain)
```

**Assessment Questions** (Ask directly!):
1. "Are you thinking about hurting yourself?"
2. "Are you thinking about suicide?"
3. "Do you have a plan?"
4. "Do you have access to means?" (guns, pills, etc.)
5. "When are you planning to do it?"
6. "What has stopped you so far?"

**NCLEX Tip**: ALWAYS ask directly about suicide. Asking does NOT plant the idea!

### 3. Anxiety Disorders

#### Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**Criteria**: Excessive worry about multiple events for 6+ months

**Physical Symptoms**:

- Fatigue
- Difficulty concentrating

- Nausea

### 6. Schizophrenia Spectrum Disorders

#### Symptoms - Two Categories

**Positive Symptoms** (Added behaviors):
- **Hallucinations**: Sensory experiences without stimulus (auditory most common)
- **Delusions**: Fixed false beliefs
- **Disorganized speech**: Word salad, loose associations, neologisms
- **Disorganized behavior**: Bizarre actions, catatonia

**Negative Symptoms** (Diminished functions):
- **Affective flattening**: Reduced emotional expression
- **Alogia**: Poverty of speech
- **Avolition**: Lack of motivation
- **Anhedonia**: Inability to experience pleasure
- **Social withdrawal**

---

#### Types of Delusions

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Persecutory** | Belief that others intend harm | "The FBI is following me" |
| **Grandiose** | Exaggerated self-importance | "I am God" |
| **Referential** | Events have special meaning for them | "TV is sending me messages" |
| **Control** | Outside force controls thoughts/actions | "Aliens control my mind" |
| **Somatic** | False belief about body | "My organs are rotting" |

---

#### Types of Hallucinations

| Type | Description | Most Common In |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Auditory** | Hearing voices/sounds | Schizophrenia |
| **Visual** | Seeing things not there | Delirium, drug intoxication |
| **Tactile** | Feeling sensations on skin | Cocaine/meth withdrawal |
| **Olfactory** | Smelling odors | Seizures, brain tumor |
| **Gustatory** | Tasting things | Rare, seizures |

## ğŸ“ Practice Questions

<details>
<summary>Question 1: A patient admitted involuntarily says "I'm leaving right now!" What should the nurse do?</summary>

**Answer**: Explain that the patient was admitted because they are a danger to themselves or others and cannot leave without a physician's order.

**Rationale**: Involuntarily committed patients cannot leave at will. They were admitted because they met criteria (danger to self, danger to others, or gravely disabled). The nurse should explain the situation calmly and involve the physician. The patient has the right to legal counsel and can challenge the commitment in court.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 2: Which patient is at HIGHEST risk for suicide?</summary>

**Answer**: 68-year-old white male with depression, previous suicide attempt, who just started antidepressants and reports improved energy.

**Rationale**: Multiple high-risk factors: male, elderly, depression, previous attempt (strongest predictor). Additionally, suicide risk INCREASES when depression begins to lift because patient regains energy to act on suicidal thoughts. This is critical time for close monitoring.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 3: A patient taking haloperidol develops severe muscle rigidity, fever of 105Â°F, and confusion. What should the nurse suspect?</summary>

**Answer**: Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS) - STOP medication immediately and notify physician.

**Rationale**: NMS is a life-threatening reaction to antipsychotics. Classic triad: fever, rigidity, altered mental status. Also see elevated CPK, unstable vitals. This is a medical emergency requiring immediate discontinuation of antipsychotic and ICU care.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 4: A patient's lithium level is 1.8 mEq/L. What should the nurse do?</summary>

**Answer**: Hold the medication and notify the physician immediately. Assess for toxicity signs.

**Rationale**: Therapeutic lithium range is 0.6-1.2 mEq/L. Levels >1.5 are toxic. At 1.8, patient is at risk for moderate toxicity (ataxia, confusion, seizures). Medication should be held and physician notified. Monitor for worsening symptoms.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 5: A patient with schizophrenia says "The FBI put a chip in my brain to control me." What is the BEST response?</summary>

**Answer**: "I understand that you believe that, but I don't see any evidence of that. You are safe here."

**Rationale**: Don't argue with delusions or reinforce them. Present reality gently without demanding patient accept it. Focus on safety and feelings rather than the delusion itself. Arguing will damage trust and is ineffective.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 6: What is the priority intervention for a patient experiencing a panic attack?</summary>

**Answer**: Stay with the patient and provide a calm, quiet environment.

**Rationale**: During panic attack, never leave patient alone. Staying calm, moving to quiet area, and using short simple sentences helps. Patient is experiencing intense fear and needs reassurance of safety. Teach breathing exercises once acute phase passes.
</details>
