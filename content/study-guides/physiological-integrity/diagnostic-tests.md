# Diagnostic Tests

## Overview
Understanding diagnostic tests, proper preparation, and interpretation is essential for the NCLEX and clinical practice. This guide covers common diagnostic procedures, nursing responsibilities, and patient preparation.

## ðŸ”¬ Common Diagnostic Procedures

### Imaging Studies

#### X-Ray (Radiography)

**Purpose**: Visualize bones, chest, abdomen

**Patient Preparation**:
- Remove jewelry, metal objects
- **Chest X-ray**: No special prep
- **Abdominal X-ray**: May require NPO, bowel prep

**Nursing Care**:
- Ensure no pregnancy (shield abdomen if necessary)
- Position patient as directed
- No specific aftercare

**Contraindications**: Pregnancy (relative - use shielding)

---

#### Computed Tomography (CT Scan)

**Purpose**: Detailed cross-sectional images

**Types**:
- **Without contrast**: Bone evaluation, bleeding detection
- **With contrast**: Organ/soft tissue evaluation

**Patient Preparation**:

**Non-contrast CT**:
- Remove metal objects
- Screen for pregnancy

**Contrast CT**:
- **NPO 4-6 hours** before procedure
- **Assess for allergies**: Iodine, shellfish, contrast dye
- **Check kidney function** (BUN, creatinine)
  - Contrast is nephrotoxic
  - Hold **metformin** 48 hours before/after (lactic acidosis risk)
- **IV access** required
- Inform patient about warm/flushing sensation

**Nursing Care After**:
- **Increase fluid intake** (flush contrast through kidneys)
- Monitor for allergic reaction
- Resume metformin only after kidney function confirmed normal
- Monitor kidney function in at-risk patients

**Contraindications**:
- Severe renal impairment (for contrast)
- Contrast allergy (use alternative or premedicate)

---

#### Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

**Purpose**: Detailed soft tissue imaging (brain, spine, joints, organs)

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Screen for metal implants**:
  - Pacemakers, ICDs (absolute contraindication)
  - Aneurysm clips
  - Cochlear implants
  - Metal fragments in eyes
  - Some joint replacements (check compatibility)
- Remove ALL metal objects (jewelry, hairpins, dentures with metal)
- **NPO not required** unless sedation planned
- **Claustrophobia screening** - may need sedation
- May require **contrast** (gadolinium) - check kidney function

**Nursing Care**:
- Explain loud knocking sounds during procedure
- Provide earplugs or music
- Explain importance of staying still
- Post-procedure: Resume normal activities

**Contraindications**:
- Pacemakers, ICDs
- Some metallic implants
- First trimester pregnancy (relative)
- Severe claustrophobia (unless sedated)

**Key Point**: **MRI uses magnets** (not radiation) - metal is dangerous!

---

#### Ultrasound (Sonography)

**Purpose**: Real-time imaging using sound waves

**Types**:
- **Abdominal**: Liver, gallbladder, kidneys, spleen
- **Pelvic**: Uterus, ovaries, bladder, prostate
- **Cardiac (Echocardiogram)**: Heart structure/function
- **Vascular (Doppler)**: Blood flow assessment

**Patient Preparation**:

| Type | Preparation |
|------|-------------|
| **Abdominal** | NPO 8-12 hours (reduce gas interference) |
| **Pelvic** | Full bladder (drink 32 oz water 1 hr before, don't void) |
| **Renal** | No special prep |
| **Echocardiogram** | No special prep |

**Nursing Care**:
- Gel applied to skin
- No radiation exposure - **safe in pregnancy**
- Patient may void immediately after pelvic ultrasound

---

#### Nuclear Medicine Scans

**Purpose**: Functional imaging using radioactive tracers

**Common Types**:
- **Bone Scan**: Detect fractures, tumors, infection
- **Thyroid Scan**: Thyroid nodules, function
- **PET Scan**: Cancer detection, brain function
- **VQ Scan**: Pulmonary embolism

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Radioactive tracer** injected IV
- **Wait period** (minutes to hours) before scanning
- **Remove metal objects**
- **PET scan**: NPO 4-6 hours, avoid exercise 24 hours before

**Nursing Care**:
- Explain radioactivity is minimal and short-lived
- **Increase fluids** after procedure (flush isotope)
- **Frequent voiding** (tracer excreted in urine)
- Use **standard precautions** - no special isolation needed
- Pregnant/breastfeeding staff should minimize exposure

**Contraindications**: Pregnancy, breastfeeding (relative)

## ðŸ«€ Cardiac Diagnostic Tests

### Electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG)

**Purpose**: Evaluate heart's electrical activity

**12-Lead ECG**:
- **No preparation** required
- Patient supine, relaxed
- Electrodes placed on chest and extremities
- Takes 5-10 minutes

**Holter Monitor** (24-48 hour continuous ECG):
- Patient keeps diary of activities/symptoms
- Avoid getting device wet
- Return after monitoring period

**Nursing Care**:
- Clean electrode sites
- Reassure patient (painless)
- Ensure patient relaxed (muscle movement causes artifact)

---

### Stress Test (Exercise Tolerance Test)

**Purpose**: Evaluate cardiac function under stress

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Light meal** 2 hours before (not NPO)
- Wear comfortable clothes and shoes
- Avoid caffeine 24 hours before
- Continue cardiac medications (unless provider specifies otherwise)
- May need to hold beta blockers (provider decision)

**Procedure**:
- Continuous ECG monitoring
- Walk on treadmill with increasing intensity
- Stop if: chest pain, severe dyspnea, ST changes, arrhythmias

**Nursing Care**:
- Monitor vital signs continuously
- Watch for chest pain, dyspnea, dizziness
- Have emergency equipment available
- Gradually cool down (don't stop abruptly)

**Contraindications**:
- Recent MI (within 2 days)
- Unstable angina
- Severe aortic stenosis
- Acute illness

---

### Cardiac Catheterization

**Purpose**: Visualize coronary arteries, measure pressures, perform interventions

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **NPO 6-8 hours** before procedure
- **Assess for allergies** (iodine, shellfish, contrast)
- **Check kidney function** (BUN, creatinine)
- **Baseline vital signs**
- Mark **pedal pulses** bilaterally
- **IV access** required
- May need to **hold anticoagulants** (per provider)
- **Void** before procedure

**Procedure**:
- Catheter inserted via femoral or radial artery
- Contrast dye injected
- Fluoroscopy to visualize arteries

**Nursing Care AFTER**:

**Immediate Post-Procedure** (first 4-6 hours):
- **Bedrest** - keep extremity straight
  - Femoral: 4-6 hours flat, leg straight
  - Radial: May sit up, wrist immobilized
- **Vital signs** every 15 min Ã— 1 hr, then per protocol
- **Assess insertion site** for bleeding, hematoma
- **Check pulses distal to site** (pedal pulses if femoral)
- **Neurovascular checks**: Color, temperature, sensation, movement
- **Monitor for complications**:
  - Bleeding/hematoma at site
  - Arrhythmias
  - Chest pain
  - Allergic reaction to contrast
- **Increase oral fluids** (flush contrast)

**Complications to Report Immediately**:
- Chest pain
- Decreased/absent pulse distal to site
- Numbness, tingling, coolness of extremity
- Significant bleeding or hematoma
- Hypotension, tachycardia (bleeding/shock)

---

### Echocardiogram (Echo)

**Purpose**: Visualize heart structure and function using ultrasound

**Types**:
- **Transthoracic (TTE)**: Standard, external
- **Transesophageal (TEE)**: Probe down esophagus

**Transthoracic Echo**:
- No preparation needed
- Gel applied to chest
- Non-invasive, painless

**Transesophageal Echo (TEE)**:
- **NPO 4-6 hours** before
- **Informed consent** required
- Throat spray (local anesthetic)
- **Conscious sedation** often used

**TEE Post-Procedure**:
- **NPO until gag reflex returns** (2-4 hours)
- Monitor for complications (rare): esophageal perforation, bleeding
- Sore throat common

## ðŸ« Pulmonary Diagnostic Tests

### Chest X-Ray

**Purpose**: Evaluate lungs, heart size, chest structures

**No preparation** needed
- Remove jewelry, metal
- Deep breath and hold during exposure
- Anteroposterior (AP) and lateral views

---

### Pulmonary Function Tests (PFTs)

**Purpose**: Measure lung volumes and capacities

**Patient Preparation**:
- Avoid bronchodilators (if testing for diagnosis)
- Avoid heavy meals before test
- No smoking 4-6 hours before
- Explain: requires maximal effort

**Types of Measurements**:
- **FVC** (Forced Vital Capacity)
- **FEV1** (Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 second)
- **FEV1/FVC ratio**

**Interpretation**:
- **Obstructive disease** (asthma, COPD): Decreased FEV1/FVC ratio
- **Restrictive disease** (fibrosis): Decreased FVC and FEV1, normal ratio

---

### Bronchoscopy

**Purpose**: Visualize airways, obtain biopsies, remove foreign objects

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **NPO 6-8 hours** before
- Remove dentures, jewelry
- **Baseline vital signs**
- Pre-procedure sedation
- **May need conscious sedation**

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **NPO until gag reflex returns** (2-4 hours)
- **Monitor for complications**:
  - Bronchospasm (wheezing)
  - Bleeding (hemoptysis)
  - **Pneumothorax** (chest pain, dyspnea, decreased breath sounds)
- **Semi-Fowler's position** or side-lying (prevent aspiration)
- Hoarseness and sore throat are expected
- Blood-tinged sputum normal initially
- Report **bright red blood** or increased bleeding

**Complications**:
- Laryngospasm
- Bronchospasm
- Pneumothorax (most serious)
- Bleeding
- Infection

---

### Thoracentesis

**Purpose**: Remove fluid from pleural space for analysis or symptom relief

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **Chest X-ray** before procedure
- Empty bladder
- **Position**: Sitting upright, leaning forward over bedside table
- **Explain**: "Don't move, cough, or deep breathe during needle insertion"

**Procedure**:
- Local anesthetic
- Needle inserted between ribs into pleural space
- Fluid withdrawn (usually 1-1.5 L max)

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **Chest X-ray** after procedure (check for pneumothorax)
- Monitor vital signs
- **Assess for complications**:
  - **Pneumothorax**: Sudden dyspnea, chest pain, decreased breath sounds
  - **Subcutaneous emphysema**: Crackling under skin
  - Bleeding
  - Infection
- Apply pressure dressing to puncture site
- **Position on unaffected side** (promotes lung expansion)

**Send fluid to lab**: Analysis for infection, malignancy

## ðŸ§  Neurological Diagnostic Tests

### Lumbar Puncture (Spinal Tap)

**Purpose**: Obtain cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), measure pressure, administer medications

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- Empty bladder
- **Position**: Side-lying, knees to chest (fetal position) OR sitting, leaning forward
- Explain procedure, importance of staying still

**Procedure**:
- Local anesthetic
- Needle inserted between L3-L4 or L4-L5
- CSF collected (opening pressure measured)

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **Flat in bed 4-12 hours** (prevent headache)
- **Encourage fluids** (replace CSF)
- Monitor for complications:
  - **Headache** (most common) - worse when upright, better lying flat
  - **Infection** (meningitis)
  - **Bleeding**
  - **Nerve damage** (numbness, tingling)
- Assess puncture site for bleeding, CSF leakage
- Monitor neurological status, vital signs

**Contraindications**:
- **Increased intracranial pressure** (risk of herniation)
- Infection at puncture site
- Severe coagulopathy

**CSF Analysis**:
- Color (clear = normal, cloudy = infection)
- Glucose (decreased in bacterial meningitis)
- Protein (elevated in infection/inflammation)
- Cell count (elevated WBC in infection)
- Culture and sensitivity

---

### Electroencephalogram (EEG)

**Purpose**: Measure brain's electrical activity (seizures, brain death)

**Patient Preparation**:
- Wash hair (no oils, sprays)
- May need to withhold anticonvulsants (provider order)
- May need **sleep deprivation** (epilepsy testing)
- Avoid caffeine before test

**Procedure**:
- Electrodes placed on scalp
- Patient may be asked to hyperventilate or view flashing lights (provoke seizure activity)
- Painless, non-invasive

**Nursing Care After**:
- Resume medications
- Wash hair (remove electrode gel)

---

### CT Head / MRI Brain

**See imaging studies section above**

**Additional Considerations**:
- **Assess neuro status** before and after
- **Stroke protocol**: CT head FIRST (rule out bleeding before tPA)
- MRI more detailed than CT but takes longer

## ðŸ”Ž Gastrointestinal Diagnostic Tests

### Endoscopy (EGD - Esophagogastroduodenoscopy)

**Purpose**: Visualize esophagus, stomach, duodenum

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **NPO 6-8 hours** before
- Remove dentures
- **IV access** for sedation
- **Baseline vital signs**

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **NPO until gag reflex returns** (2-4 hours)
- **Position on side** (prevent aspiration)
- Monitor for complications:
  - **Perforation** (severe pain, fever, subcutaneous emphysema)
  - Bleeding
  - Aspiration
- Report: severe pain, difficulty swallowing, bleeding

---

### Colonoscopy

**Purpose**: Visualize large intestine, remove polyps, biopsy

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **Bowel preparation** (most important!):
  - Clear liquid diet 24-48 hours before
  - **Laxatives/cathartics** (GoLYTELY, MiraLAX)
  - **NPO midnight** before procedure (except medications)
- **IV access** for sedation
- Explain: cramping and urge to defecate are normal

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- Monitor vital signs
- Assess for complications:
  - **Perforation** (abdominal pain, distention, fever)
  - **Bleeding** (if polyp removed)
- **Flatulence is expected** (encourage passing gas)
- Resume diet as tolerated
- Report: severe abdominal pain, rectal bleeding, fever

**Key Point**: **Quality of bowel prep = quality of examination!**

---

### Barium Swallow / Upper GI Series

**Purpose**: Visualize esophagus, stomach, small intestine

**Patient Preparation**:
- **NPO 8-12 hours** before
- Remove metal objects
- Explain: will drink chalky barium contrast

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **Increase fluids** (prevent constipation)
- **Laxative** may be ordered
- Stools will be **white/clay-colored** for 24-72 hours (barium)
- Monitor for **constipation/impaction**

---

### Barium Enema

**Purpose**: Visualize large intestine

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Bowel prep** (laxatives, clear liquids)
- **NPO midnight** before
- Explain: barium instilled via rectum, cramping expected

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- Same as barium swallow
- **Increase fluids, laxative**
- **White stools** for 1-3 days
- Monitor for constipation

---

### Liver Biopsy

**Purpose**: Diagnose liver disease, cirrhosis, cancer

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **NPO 4-6 hours** before (reduce aspiration risk if sedation used)
- **Check coagulation studies** (PT, aPTT, platelets)
  - Hold anticoagulants
  - May need vitamin K or platelets
- **Type and crossmatch** blood (in case of bleeding)
- **Baseline vital signs**
- Empty bladder
- **Position**: Supine or left lateral

**Procedure**:
- Local anesthetic
- Needle inserted through right intercostal space
- Patient holds breath on expiration during needle insertion

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **Position on RIGHT side** for 2-4 hours (apply pressure to biopsy site)
- **Bedrest** for 24 hours
- **Monitor vital signs** frequently (q15min Ã— 2hrs)
- **Assess for bleeding**:
  - Hypotension, tachycardia
  - Abdominal pain
  - Shoulder pain (referred pain from diaphragmatic irritation)
- **No heavy lifting, straining** for 1 week

**Complications**:
- **Hemorrhage** (most serious)
- Bile peritonitis
- Pneumothorax

---

## ðŸ©¸ Bone Marrow Biopsy/Aspiration

**Purpose**: Diagnose blood disorders, leukemia, lymphoma

**Common Sites**: Posterior iliac crest (most common), sternum

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **Check coagulation studies**
- Explain: pressure and brief sharp pain during aspiration
- **Position**: Side-lying or prone (iliac crest)

**Procedure**:
- Local anesthetic (skin/periosteum)
- Needle inserted into bone
- Marrow aspirated or core biopsy obtained

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **Apply pressure** 5-10 minutes (longer if coagulopathy)
- **Pressure dressing** applied
- Monitor for bleeding
- Pain management (site tenderness common)
- May resume normal activities
- Report: excessive bleeding, severe pain, fever

## ðŸ’§ Urological Diagnostic Tests

### Urinalysis

**Purpose**: Screen for UTI, kidney disease, diabetes

**Collection Methods**:

| Method | Description | When Used |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Random** | Any time collection | Routine screening |
| **Clean-Catch Midstream** | After perineal cleaning | Culture (minimize contamination) |
| **Catheterized** | Via catheter | If patient cannot void |
| **24-Hour** | All urine for 24 hours | Protein, creatinine clearance |

**24-Hour Urine Collection**:
1. **Discard first void** (start time)
2. Collect ALL urine for next 24 hours
3. Keep refrigerated or on ice
4. Last void at exactly 24 hours after start

---

### Cystoscopy

**Purpose**: Visualize bladder, obtain biopsies, remove stones

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- May require **bowel prep**
- **IV access** (for sedation if general anesthesia)
- **Force fluids** before procedure (unless contraindicated)

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **Monitor voiding** - first void after procedure
- **Expect**: Pink-tinged urine, burning on urination
- **Increase fluid intake** (flush bladder)
- **Warm sitz baths** for discomfort
- Monitor for complications:
  - **Urinary retention**
  - **UTI** (fever, dysuria, frequency)
  - **Bleeding** (bright red urine)
  - **Perforation** (severe pain)

---

### Intravenous Pyelogram (IVP)

**Purpose**: Visualize kidneys, ureters, bladder using contrast

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- **Bowel prep** (laxatives)
- **NPO 8-12 hours** before
- **Assess allergies** (iodine, shellfish, contrast)
- **Check kidney function** (BUN, creatinine)
- **Hydration** before procedure

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- **Increase fluids** (flush contrast)
- Monitor for allergic reaction
- Resume normal diet

## ðŸ§¬ Genetic/Prenatal Testing

### Amniocentesis

**Purpose**: Genetic testing, lung maturity, chromosomal abnormalities

**Timing**: Usually 15-20 weeks gestation (genetic) or third trimester (lung maturity)

**Patient Preparation**:
- **Informed consent** required
- Empty bladder (if >20 weeks) OR full bladder (if <20 weeks)
- **Ultrasound** to locate placenta and fetus
- **Baseline fetal heart rate**

**Procedure**:
- Local anesthetic
- Needle inserted through abdomen into amniotic sac
- Fluid withdrawn

**Nursing Care AFTER**:
- Monitor **fetal heart rate**
- Assess for:
  - Cramping (mild expected)
  - Vaginal bleeding or fluid leakage
  - Fever
  - Decreased fetal movement
- **RhoGAM** if mother Rh-negative
- Rest for 24 hours
- Report: bleeding, cramping, fluid leakage, fever

## ðŸŽ¯ NCLEX Tips

### General Principles for All Diagnostic Tests

**Before the Test**:
1. **Informed consent** - invasive procedures require signed consent
2. **Allergies** - especially iodine/shellfish for contrast studies
3. **NPO status** - verify fasting requirements
4. **Baseline vital signs and assessment**
5. **Patient teaching** - explain procedure, what to expect
6. **Remove contraindicated items** (metal for MRI, jewelry for X-ray)

**After the Test**:
1. **Monitor vital signs** - especially after invasive procedures
2. **Assess for complications** - bleeding, infection, perforation
3. **Site care** - if puncture/incision
4. **Activity restrictions** - bedrest, limitations
5. **Resume diet/medications** - when appropriate

### High-Yield NCLEX Points

1. **NPO for sedation** = 6-8 hours
2. **Gag reflex must return** before eating/drinking after throat procedures
3. **Check kidney function** before contrast studies
4. **Hold metformin** 48 hours before/after contrast
5. **Increase fluids** after contrast (flush through kidneys)
6. **Mark pulses** before cardiac cath (compare after)
7. **Position on right side** after liver biopsy (tamponade bleeding)
8. **Flat in bed** after lumbar puncture (prevent headache)
9. **Quality bowel prep** = quality colonoscopy
10. **Informed consent required** for invasive procedures

### Priority Complications

**Report immediately**:
- **Pneumothorax**: After thoracentesis, bronchoscopy, lung biopsy
- **Bleeding**: After liver biopsy, cardiac cath, bone marrow biopsy
- **Perforation**: After colonoscopy, endoscopy
- **Allergic reaction**: After contrast administration
- **Cardiac events**: After stress test, cardiac cath

## ðŸ“ Practice Questions

<details>
<summary>Question 1: A patient is scheduled for an MRI. Which finding requires intervention before the test?</summary>

**Answer**: Patient has a pacemaker

**Rationale**: Pacemakers are an ABSOLUTE contraindication to MRI due to the strong magnetic field. The magnet can cause pacemaker malfunction. Other metal implants need to be verified for MRI compatibility. Always screen for metal before MRI.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 2: After a liver biopsy, in which position should the patient be placed?</summary>

**Answer**: Right side-lying position

**Rationale**: Positioning on the right side applies pressure to the biopsy site and helps prevent bleeding. Patient should remain in this position for 2-4 hours, then bedrest for 24 hours. Monitor for bleeding (hypotension, tachycardia, abdominal/shoulder pain).
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 3: A patient returns from cardiac catheterization via right femoral artery. What is the priority assessment?</summary>

**Answer**: Check right pedal pulse and compare to left

**Rationale**: After cardiac cath, priority is monitoring neurovascular status distal to insertion site. Check pedal pulses (should have been marked pre-procedure), color, temperature, sensation, movement. Decreased or absent pulse indicates arterial occlusion - a medical emergency.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 4: When can a patient eat after a bronchoscopy?</summary>

**Answer**: After the gag reflex returns (2-4 hours)

**Rationale**: The throat is anesthetized during bronchoscopy. Patient must remain NPO until gag reflex returns to prevent aspiration. Test gag reflex before offering food or fluids. This applies to ANY procedure involving throat anesthesia (EGD, TEE, bronchoscopy).
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 5: A patient taking metformin is scheduled for a CT scan with contrast. What should the nurse do?</summary>

**Answer**: Hold metformin 48 hours before and after the procedure

**Rationale**: Metformin combined with IV contrast can cause lactic acidosis. Hold metformin 48 hours before and 48 hours after contrast administration. Resume only after kidney function (creatinine) is verified as normal. Also ensure adequate hydration.
</details>
