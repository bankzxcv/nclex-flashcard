## ðŸ’‰ Coagulation Studies

| Test | Normal Range | Purpose |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **PT (Prothrombin Time)** | 11-13 seconds | Monitor Warfarin therapy |
| **INR (International Normalized Ratio)** | 0.8-1.2<br>Therapeutic: 2.0-3.0 | Standardized PT for anticoagulation |

- **PT/INR monitoring**: Check before Warfarin administration
- **aPTT monitoring**: Check before Heparin dose
- **Bleeding precautions**: Soft toothbrush, electric razor, avoid IM injections
- **Antidotes**: Vitamin K for Warfarin, Protamine sulfate for Heparin

- Assess for jaundice, dark urine, pale stools
- Monitor for bleeding (decreased clotting factors)
- Avoid hepatotoxic medications
- Low protein diet if ammonia elevated

## ðŸ”´ Arterial Blood Gases (ABG)

| Component | Normal Range | Represents |
|-----------|--------------|------------|
| **pH** | 7.35-7.45 | Acid-base balance |
| **PaCO2** | 35-45 mmHg | Respiratory component |
| **HCO3-** | 22-26 mEq/L | Metabolic component |
| **PaO2** | 80-100 mmHg | Oxygenation |
| **SaO2** | 95-100% | Oxygen saturation |

### ABG Interpretation Steps

1. **Check pH**: Acidosis (<7.35) or Alkalosis (>7.45)?
2. **Check PaCO2**: Respiratory cause?
   - High CO2 = Respiratory Acidosis
   - Low CO2 = Respiratory Alkalosis
3. **Check HCO3-**: Metabolic cause?
   - Low HCO3- = Metabolic Acidosis
   - High HCO3- = Metabolic Alkalosis
4. **Determine compensation**: Is the body trying to correct?

2. **Remember normal ranges**: 3.5-5.0 for K+, 135-145 for Na+
3. **Know critical values**: These require immediate notification
4. **Understand relationships**: TSH â†‘ = T4 â†“

- Focus on **abnormal** values and nursing interventions

- Understand what affects lab values (medications, diet, hydration)
- Recognize critical values requiring immediate action
- Know antidotes and reversal agents

## ðŸ“ Practice Questions

<details>
<summary>Question 1: A client's lab shows K+ = 6.2 mEq/L. What is the priority nursing action?</summary>

**Answer**: Assess cardiac rhythm and notify provider immediately.

**Rationale**: Hyperkalemia (>5.0 mEq/L) can cause life-threatening cardiac arrhythmias. Priority is cardiac monitoring and treatment (calcium gluconate, insulin with dextrose, kayexalate).
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 2: A client on Warfarin has an INR of 4.5. What should the nurse do?</summary>

**Answer**: Hold the dose and notify the provider.

**Rationale**: Therapeutic INR is 2.0-3.0. A value of 4.5 indicates increased bleeding risk. The dose should be held and provider notified. Assess for bleeding signs.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 3: ABG results: pH 7.30, PaCO2 50, HCO3- 24. What is this?</summary>

**Answer**: Respiratory Acidosis (uncompensated)

**Rationale**: pH is acidotic (<7.35), PaCO2 is high (>45), indicating respiratory cause. HCO3- is normal, so no compensation yet. Common in COPD or respiratory depression.
</details>
