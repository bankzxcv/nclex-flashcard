# Physical Assessment Techniques

## Overview

Comprehensive physical assessment is fundamental to nursing practice and frequently tested on the NCLEX. This guide covers head-to-toe assessment techniques, normal and abnormal findings, and assessment modifications across the lifespan. Mastering these skills is essential for safe, effective patient care.

### Inspection

**Definition**: Systematic visual examination

**Technique**:
- Good lighting essential
- Expose area fully (maintain privacy)
- Compare bilateral structures (symmetry)
- Note color, size, shape, position, symmetry

**What to Observe**:
- Skin color, lesions
- Symmetry
- Movement, pulsations
- Drainage, discharge
- Deformities, edema

**NCLEX Tip**: Inspection is ALWAYS first step in assessment (except trauma - ABCs first)

---

### Palpation

**Definition**: Using hands to feel body surfaces and organs

**Types**:

#### Light Palpation
- **Depth**: ~1 cm (Â½ inch)
- **Technique**: Fingertips, gentle pressure
- **Purpose**: Surface characteristics, tenderness, temperature, moisture

#### Deep Palpation
- **Depth**: 4-5 cm (2 inches)
- **Technique**: Use both hands (bimanual), more pressure
- **Purpose**: Organ size, shape, masses
- **Caution**: Avoid in acute abdomen, suspected appendicitis, transplanted organs

### Percussion

**Definition**: Tapping body surface to assess underlying structures by sound produced

**Technique**:
- Non-dominant hand: Place firmly on body (pleximeter)
- Dominant hand: Use middle finger to strike (plexor)
- Strike with quick, sharp blow at joint (not fingertip)
- Wrist movement only (not whole arm)

**Percussion Sounds**:

| Sound | Intensity | Pitch | Duration | Quality | Example Location |
|-------|-----------|-------|----------|---------|-----------------|
| **Resonance** | Loud | Low | Long | Hollow | Normal lung |
| **Hyperresonance** | Very loud | Very low | Longer | Booming | Emphysema, pneumothorax |
| **Tympany** | Loud | High | Medium | Drum-like | Gastric air bubble, distended abdomen |
| **Dull** | Soft | High | Short | Thud | Liver, heart, consolidated lung |
| **Flat** | Very soft | High | Very short | Flat | Muscle, bone |

**NCLEX Tip**:
- **Lung**: Resonance = normal, Dull = pneumonia/fluid, Hyperresonance = pneumothorax/emphysema
- **Abdomen**: Tympany = normal (air), Dull = mass/fluid

- Low-pitched sounds
- Heart murmurs (S3, S4), bruits

### Head and Face

#### Inspection
- **Shape**: Normocephalic (normal shape)
- **Symmetry**: Facial features symmetric
- **Skin**: Color, lesions, hair distribution
- **Movement**: Facial expressions, symmetry when smiling

#### Palpation
- **Skull**: Smooth, no masses, tenderness
- **Temporal artery**: Palpate for pulsations
- **TMJ**: Smooth movement, no clicking or pain
- **Sinuses**: Frontal and maxillary, no tenderness

**Cranial Nerve Assessment** (face):
- **CN V (Trigeminal)**: Facial sensation, clench jaw
- **CN VII (Facial)**: Smile, frown, close eyes tight

**Abnormal Findings**:
- Facial asymmetry (stroke, Bell's palsy)
- Sinus tenderness (sinusitis)
- TMJ clicking (TMJ disorder)

---

### Eyes

#### Visual Acuity
- **Snellen chart**: 20/20 normal
- **Near vision**: Reading card

#### External Structures

**Inspection**:
- **Eyebrows**: Symmetric
- **Eyelids**: Symmetric, no drooping (ptosis)
- **Eyelashes**: Present, no crusting
- **Conjunctiva**: Pink, moist (pull down lower lid)
- **Sclera**: White (icteric = yellow = jaundice)
- **Pupils**: PERRLA (Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation)

**PERRLA Assessment**:
```
1. Size: Equal (2-6 mm)
2. Shape: Round
3. Light reflex: Constrict when light shined
   - Direct: Same eye constricts
   - Consensual: Opposite eye constricts
4. Accommodation: Converge and constrict when
   focus changes from far to near
```

**Abnormal Pupils**:
- **Unequal**: Increased ICP, stroke, eye trauma
- **Pinpoint**: Opioid overdose
- **Dilated**: Anticholinergic drugs, brain death

#### Extraocular Movements (EOM)

**Six Cardinal Fields of Gaze**:
- Patient follows finger in H pattern
- Assess: CN III (Oculomotor), CN IV (Trochlear), CN VI (Abducens)
- Normal: Smooth tracking, no nystagmus

**Mnemonic**: "**CN 3, 4, 6 make your eyes do tricks**"

---

### Ears

#### External Ear

**Inspection**:
- Position, size, symmetry
- No lesions, discharge

**Palpation**:
- Auricle, mastoid process (no tenderness)
- **Pain with palpation** = external ear infection (otitis externa)
- **Mastoid tenderness** = mastoiditis

#### Otoscopic Examination

**Technique**:
1. Pull pinna up and back (adults) or down and back (children <3)
2. Insert speculum gently
3. Assess canal and tympanic membrane (TM)

**Normal Tympanic Membrane**:
- Pearly gray color
- Intact, translucent
- Cone of light visible (5 o'clock right ear, 7 o'clock left ear)
- Landmarks visible

**Abnormal**:
- **Red, bulging TM** = Acute otitis media
- **Retracted TM** = Eustachian tube dysfunction
- **Amber fluid** = Serous otitis media
- **Perforation** = Chronic infection, trauma

#### Hearing

**Whisper Test**: Whisper 1-2 feet away, patient repeats

**Weber Test** (tuning fork on midline skull):
- Normal: Sound equal both ears
- Abnormal: Sound lateralizes to affected ear (conductive loss) or unaffected ear (sensorineural loss)

**Rinne Test** (tuning fork on mastoid then near ear):
- Normal: Air conduction > Bone conduction (AC > BC)
- Conductive loss: BC > AC

### Nose and Sinuses

#### Nose

**Inspection**:
- External: Symmetry, no deformity
- Internal (with light): Nasal mucosa pink, septum midline, no polyps

**Abnormal**:
- **Deviated septum**: Asymmetry
- **Pale, boggy mucosa**: Allergies
- **Red, swollen mucosa**: Infection
- **Epistaxis**: Nosebleed

#### Sinuses

**Palpation/Percussion**:
- Frontal sinuses (above eyebrows)
- Maxillary sinuses (below eyes, lateral to nose)

**Normal**: No tenderness

**Abnormal**: Tenderness, pain = sinusitis

### Mouth and Throat

#### Lips
- Pink, moist, symmetric
- No lesions, cyanosis, pallor

#### Buccal Mucosa
- Pink, moist
- No lesions, white patches (candidiasis)

#### Gums/Teeth
- Pink gums, no bleeding, recession
- Teeth present, condition

#### Tongue
- **CN XII (Hypoglossal)**: Stick out tongue (midline, no deviation)
- Pink, moist, papillae present
- No coating (white coating = candidiasis)

#### Palate
- Hard palate: Anterior, bony
- Soft palate: Posterior, mobile

#### Uvula
- **CN IX, X (Glossopharyngeal, Vagus)**: Say "Ahh" - uvula midline, rises
- Gag reflex present

#### Tonsils
- Present, not enlarged
- No exudate (white/yellow = strep throat)

#### Throat
- Pink, moist
- No erythema, exudate

### Neck

#### Inspection
- Symmetric
- Trachea midline
- No masses, scars

#### Palpation

**Lymph Nodes**:
- Preauricular, postauricular
- Occipital
- Submandibular, submental
- Anterior cervical, posterior cervical
- Supraclavicular (enlarged = cancer concern)

**Normal**: Non-palpable or small, soft, mobile, non-tender

**Abnormal**: Enlarged, hard, fixed, tender = infection, malignancy

**Thyroid**:
- Stand behind patient
- Palpate while patient swallows (gland rises)
- Normal: Small, smooth, rises with swallowing
- Abnormal: Enlarged (goiter), nodules

**Carotid Arteries**:
- Palpate ONE side at a time (never both simultaneously - could cut off blood flow to brain)
- Strong, equal pulses
- **Auscultate** for bruits (whooshing sound = stenosis)

**Range of Motion**:
- Flexion/extension
- Lateral flexion (ear to shoulder)
- Rotation (turn head side to side)

### Thorax and Lungs

#### Inspection

**From Front and Back**:
- **Shape**: Symmetric, AP:lateral diameter 1:2
- **Breathing pattern**: Effortless, regular, 12-20/min
- **Skin**: Color, lesions, scars
- **Movement**: Symmetric chest expansion

**Abnormal**:
- **Barrel chest** (AP = lateral): COPD, aging
- **Use of accessory muscles**: Respiratory distress
- **Asymmetric expansion**: Pneumothorax, atelectasis, pneumonia

#### Palpation

**Chest Expansion**:
- Hands on posterior chest at 10th rib
- Thumbs meet at midline
- Patient takes deep breath
- Thumbs should move apart equally

**Tactile Fremitus**:
- Patient says "ninety-nine"
- Palpate vibrations
- Normal: Equal bilaterally
- **Increased**: Pneumonia, consolidation
- **Decreased**: Pneumothorax, pleural effusion, emphysema

#### Percussion

**Technique**: Percuss posterior chest in systematic pattern

**Normal**: Resonance over lung fields, dull over organs (liver, heart)

**Abnormal**:
- **Hyperresonance**: Pneumothorax, emphysema, asthma
- **Dull**: Pneumonia, atelectasis, pleural effusion, tumor

**Diaphragmatic Excursion**:
- Percuss level of diaphragm on full exhalation and inhalation
- Normal: 3-5 cm difference

#### Auscultation

**Technique**:
- Systematic pattern: Apex to base, side to side
- Patient breathes deeply through mouth
- Listen full inspiratory and expiratory cycle

**Normal Breath Sounds**:

| Type | Location | I:E Ratio | Quality |
|------|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Bronchial** | Trachea | E > I | Loud, harsh, high-pitched |
| **Bronchovesicular** | Between scapulae, 1st-2nd ICS | I = E | Medium intensity |
| **Vesicular** | Peripheral lung | I > E | Soft, low-pitched |

**Abnormal (Adventitious) Breath Sounds**:

| Sound | Characteristics | Cause | Examples |
|-------|----------------|-------|----------|
| **Crackles (Rales)** | Popping, crackling, discontinuous | Fluid in airways/alveoli | Pneumonia, CHF, atelectasis |
| **Wheezes** | Musical, continuous, high-pitched | Narrowed airways | Asthma, COPD, bronchitis |
| **Rhonchi** | Low-pitched, snoring, continuous | Secretions in large airways | Bronchitis, mucus |
| **Stridor** | Loud, high-pitched, inspiratory | Upper airway obstruction | Croup, foreign body, epiglottitis |
| **Pleural Friction Rub** | Grating, rubbing | Inflamed pleural surfaces | Pleuritis, pneumonia |

**Voice Sounds** (if abnormality suspected):
- **Bronchophony**: Say "ninety-nine" - words clear = consolidation
- **Egophony**: Say "eee" - sounds like "ay" = consolidation
- **Whispered Pectoriloquy**: Whisper "one-two-three" - words clear = consolidation

**Normal Heart Sounds**:
- **S1** ("lub"): Closure of AV valves (mitral, tricuspid) - Beginning of systole
- **S2** ("dub"): Closure of semilunar valves (aortic, pulmonic) - Beginning of diastole
- **Normal**: S1-S2, S1-S2 (lub-dub, lub-dub)

**Extra Heart Sounds**:
- **S3** (ventricular gallop): Normal in children/young adults; ABNORMAL in adults >30 = fluid overload, CHF
- **S4** (atrial gallop): ALWAYS abnormal = hypertension, CAD, stiff ventricle
- **Murmurs**: Turbulent blood flow through valves

**Grading Murmurs**:
- **Grade I**: Barely audible
- **Grade II**: Quiet but clearly audible
- **Grade III**: Moderately loud
- **Grade IV**: Loud with palpable thrill
- **Grade V**: Very loud, stethoscope partially off chest
- **Grade VI**: Audible with stethoscope off chest

### Peripheral Vascular System

#### Inspection
- Color: Pink, no cyanosis, pallor
- Edema: None
- Hair distribution: Present (indicates good circulation)
- Varicosities: Tortuous veins

#### Palpation

**Pulses** (rate, rhythm, amplitude):
- **Temporal**
- **Carotid** (one side at a time!)
- **Brachial**
- **Radial**
- **Femoral**
- **Popliteal** (behind knee, patient prone or supine with knee flexed)
- **Dorsalis pedis** (top of foot)
- **Posterior tibial** (behind medial malleolus)

**Pulse Grading**:
- **0**: Absent
- **1+**: Diminished, weak
- **2+**: Normal
- **3+**: Full, increased
- **4+**: Bounding

**Capillary Refill**:
- Press nail bed until blanches
- Release, count seconds until color returns
- Normal: <2-3 seconds
- Delayed: Poor perfusion

**Edema Grading**:
- **1+**: Mild, 2mm depression
- **2+**: Moderate, 4mm depression
- **3+**: Deep, 6mm depression, lasts >1 minute
- **4+**: Very deep, 8mm depression, lasts 2-5 minutes

**Temperature**: Warm, equal bilaterally

**Special Tests**:

**Homan's Sign** (NOT routinely done - unreliable):
- Dorsiflex foot
- Pain in calf = possible DVT
- **NCLEX**: If positive, do NOT massage (risk of embolus)

**Allen's Test** (before arterial blood gas from radial artery):
- Occlude radial and ulnar arteries
- Patient makes fist then opens
- Release ulnar artery
- Palm should flush pink (ulnar artery patent)

### Abdomen

**REMEMBER**: Auscultate BEFORE palpation (I-A-P-P)

#### Inspection

**Skin**:
- Color, scars, striae, lesions
- No distention, masses

**Contour**:
- Flat, rounded, or scaphoid (sunken)
- **Distended** = Ascites, obstruction, pregnancy

**Umbilicus**:
- Midline, inverted
- **Cullen's sign** (blue around umbilicus) = Intraperitoneal hemorrhage
- **Grey Turner's sign** (blue flanks) = Retroperitoneal hemorrhage

**Movement**:
- Symmetric with breathing
- Visible peristalsis (abnormal except very thin)
- Visible pulsations (AAA concern if prominent)

#### Auscultation

**Bowel Sounds**:
- All four quadrants
- Listen at least 1 minute per quadrant before declaring absent
- Normal: Gurgles, clicks, 5-30/minute

**Abnormal**:
- **Hyperactive**: Diarrhea, early obstruction
- **Hypoactive/Absent**: Paralytic ileus, peritonitis, post-surgery
- **High-pitched, tinkling**: Intestinal obstruction

**Vascular Sounds**:
- **Bruits**: Listen over aorta, renal arteries, iliac arteries
- Presence = stenosis

#### Percussion

**Technique**: All four quadrants

**Normal**: Tympany (air) over most of abdomen, dull over liver, spleen

**Liver Span**:
- Percuss from below umbilicus up to dullness (lower border)
- Percuss from lung resonance down to dullness (upper border)
- Measure distance
- Normal: 6-12 cm at midclavicular line

**Ascites Assessment**:
- **Fluid wave**: Assistant places hand at midline, tap one side, feel wave on opposite side
- **Shifting dullness**: Percuss with patient supine (dull flanks), then side-lying (dullness shifts)

#### Palpation

**Light Palpation**:
- All four quadrants
- Assess for tenderness, masses, guarding

**Deep Palpation**:
- Organ size, masses
- **Caution**: Avoid in acute abdomen, appendicitis

**Specific Assessments**:

**Liver**:
- Place hand below right costal margin
- Patient takes deep breath
- Liver edge may be felt (smooth, firm edge normal)

**Spleen**:
- Place hand below left costal margin
- Patient takes deep breath
- **Not normally palpable**
- Palpable = Splenomegaly

**Kidneys**:
- Bimanual palpation
- **Not normally palpable** (except right kidney in thin individuals)

**Rebound Tenderness**:
- Press deeply then quickly release
- **Pain on release** = Peritoneal inflammation (appendicitis, peritonitis)
- **Do this test LAST** (causes pain)

**McBurney's Point** (appendicitis):
- Right lower quadrant
- Midpoint between umbilicus and right anterior superior iliac spine
- Tenderness = appendicitis

**Murphy's Sign** (cholecystitis):
- Palpate below right costal margin
- Patient takes deep breath
- **Sudden stop in breathing due to pain** = Positive (gallbladder inflammation)

### Musculoskeletal System

#### Inspection
- Posture, gait
- Symmetry, alignment
- Muscle size, symmetry
- Joint size, swelling, deformity
- Skin color, lesions

#### Palpation
- Muscle tone, strength
- Joint: Temperature, swelling, tenderness, crepitus
- Bone: Tenderness, deformities

#### Range of Motion (ROM)

**Active**: Patient moves independently
**Passive**: Nurse moves joint (assess if active limited)

**Assess Each Joint**:
- Full ROM without pain, crepitus
- Compare bilateral

**Abnormal**:
- Limited ROM
- Pain with movement
- Crepitus (grinding sensation)
- Instability

#### Muscle Strength

**Grading**:
- **5/5**: Full ROM against full resistance (normal)
- **4/5**: Full ROM against moderate resistance
- **3/5**: Full ROM against gravity only
- **2/5**: Full ROM with gravity eliminated (passive)
- **1/5**: Slight contraction, no movement
- **0/5**: No contraction

**Assess**:
- Hand grips (equal, strong)
- Plantar flexion (push feet against resistance)
- Dorsiflexion (pull feet up against resistance)

**Special Tests**:

**Phalen's Test** (carpal tunnel):
- Flex wrists (back of hands together) for 60 seconds
- Numbness, tingling = Positive

**Tinel's Sign** (carpal tunnel):
- Tap median nerve at wrist
- Tingling in fingers = Positive

| **IV** | Trochlear | Eye movement (down, in) | EOM |
| **V** | Trigeminal | Facial sensation, chewing | Touch face, clench jaw |
| **VI** | Abducens | Eye movement (lateral) | EOM |
| **VII** | Facial | Facial movement, taste | Smile, frown, close eyes |
| **VIII** | Acoustic | Hearing, balance | Whisper test, Weber, Rinne |
| **IX** | Glossopharyngeal | Taste, swallow, gag | Say "ahh", gag reflex |
| **X** | Vagus | Swallow, voice | Say "ahh", voice quality |
| **XI** | Spinal Accessory | Shoulder shrug, head turn | Shrug against resistance |
| **XII** | Hypoglossal | Tongue movement | Stick out tongue (midline) |

---

#### Motor Function

**Gait**: Smooth, coordinated, steady

**Coordination**:
- **Finger to nose**: Touch nurse's finger, then own nose
- **Rapid alternating movements**: Pat knees alternating hands
- **Heel to shin**: Run heel down opposite shin

**Balance**:
- **Romberg test**: Stand with feet together, eyes closed, arms at side
  - Normal: Slight swaying
  - Abnormal: Loss of balance (cerebellar problem, inner ear problem)

---

#### Sensory Function

**Light Touch**: Cotton wisp on arms, legs (eyes closed)

**Pain**: Pinprick (or dull vs sharp)

**Temperature**: Hot vs cold

**Vibration**: Tuning fork on bony prominences

**Position Sense** (Proprioception): Move finger/toe up or down, patient identifies

**Compare**: Bilateral, distal to proximal

---

#### Reflexes

**Deep Tendon Reflexes** (DTRs):

**Grading**:
- **0**: No response
- **1+**: Diminished, hypoactive
- **2+**: Normal
- **3+**: Brisk, hyperactive
- **4+**: Very brisk, hyperactive, clonus

**Common Reflexes**:
- **Biceps**: C5-C6
- **Triceps**: C7-C8
- **Brachioradialis**: C5-C6
- **Patellar**: L2-L4
- **Achilles**: S1-S2

**Abnormal**:
- **Hyperactive**: Upper motor neuron lesion, hypocalcemia
- **Hypoactive/Absent**: Lower motor neuron lesion, hypermagnesemia

**Superficial Reflexes**:

**Plantar (Babinski)**:
- Stroke lateral sole of foot
- Normal (adult): Toes curl down
- **Babinski positive**: Big toe extends, toes fan out
  - Normal in infants <12 months
  - ABNORMAL in adults = Upper motor neuron lesion, CNS damage

**Abdominal**: Stroke abdomen, umbilicus moves toward stroke

## ğŸ©º NCLEX Tips

### High-Yield Assessment Facts

1. **Order**: I-P-P-A (EXCEPT abdomen: I-A-P-P)
2. **Compare bilaterally**: Pulses, breath sounds, reflexes
3. **Most important vital sign in shock**: Blood pressure
4. **First assessment in emergency**: ABCs (Airway, Breathing, Circulation)
5. **S3 heart sound**: Normal in children; CHF in adults
6. **JVD**: Assess at 30-45Â° angle
7. **Never palpate both carotids** simultaneously
8. **Babinski positive in adults**: CNS damage
9. **Rebound tenderness**: Peritoneal inflammation
10. **Late decelerations**: Fetal hypoxia - intervene!

### Common NCLEX Scenarios

**Scenario**: Which assessment finding requires immediate action?
- Look for: ABCs, life-threatening conditions (airway obstruction, severe bleeding, unequal pupils)

**Scenario**: What is the first assessment?
- Usually: Airway and breathing (exceptions: conscious patient, routine admission)

**Scenario**: Nurse notes abnormal finding. What should nurse do first?
- Usually: Further assess, gather more data (unless life-threatening)

## âœ… Practice Questions

<details>
<summary>Question 1: When assessing the abdomen, in what order should the nurse perform the assessment techniques?</summary>

**Answer**: Inspection, Auscultation, Percussion, Palpation (I-A-P-P)

**Rationale**: For abdominal assessment, auscultate BEFORE palpation because palpation can alter bowel sounds. This is the ONLY body system where auscultation comes before palpation. For all other systems, use I-P-P-A order.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 2: A patient has crackles in lung bases bilaterally. Which condition is most likely?</summary>

**Answer**: Heart failure (fluid in alveoli)

**Rationale**: Crackles (rales) are discontinuous, popping sounds caused by fluid in airways/alveoli. Bilateral basilar crackles are classic for heart failure (fluid overload). Unilateral crackles may indicate pneumonia. Wheezes = narrowed airways (asthma). Rhonchi = secretions in large airways.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 3: Nurse palpates pedal pulses as 1+ bilaterally. What does this indicate?</summary>

**Answer**: Diminished, weak pulses bilaterally

**Rationale**: Pulse grading: 0=absent, 1+=diminished, 2+=normal, 3+=increased, 4+=bounding. 1+ pulses indicate decreased peripheral circulation. Further assess: color, temperature, capillary refill, pain. May indicate arterial insufficiency. Compare to previous assessments.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 4: When assessing PERRLA, what does the "A" represent?</summary>

**Answer**: Accommodation (pupils constrict and eyes converge when focus changes from far to near)

**Rationale**: PERRLA = Pupils Equal, Round, Reactive to Light and Accommodation. Accommodation tests CN III (oculomotor). Patient focuses on distant object, then nurse's finger close to nose. Normal: pupils constrict, eyes converge. Tests near vision coordination.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 5: A patient has a positive Romberg test. What does this indicate?</summary>

**Answer**: Loss of balance when standing with eyes closed, indicating cerebellar or proprioceptive problem.

**Rationale**: Romberg test assesses balance, cerebellar function, proprioception. Patient stands with feet together, arms at side, eyes closed. Normal = slight swaying. Positive = loss of balance, must step to catch self. Indicates problem with cerebellum, posterior columns (spinal cord), or vestibular system. Fall precautions needed!
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 6: Which finding requires immediate intervention?</summary>

**Answer**: Unequal pupils in trauma patient

**Rationale**: Unequal pupils (anisocoria) after head trauma indicates increased intracranial pressure, herniation - MEDICAL EMERGENCY. Notify provider immediately. Other concerning findings: decreased LOC, widening pulse pressure, bradycardia (Cushing's triad). Position HOB 30Â°, prevent Valsalva, prepare for interventions.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 7: Nurse notes JVD at 5 cm above sternal angle with patient at 45Â°. What does this indicate?</summary>

**Answer**: Elevated JVD indicates fluid overload or right-sided heart failure.

**Rationale**: Normal JVD â‰¤3 cm above sternal angle at 45Â°. Elevated JVD indicates increased venous pressure from fluid overload, right heart failure, cardiac tamponade, pulmonary hypertension. Assess for other signs: edema, weight gain, dyspnea, crackles. May need diuretics.
</details>

**Answer**: Severe brain injury, coma (score â‰¤8)

**Rationale**: GCS scoring: 13-15=mild injury, 9-12=moderate injury, â‰¤8=severe injury/coma. GCS 7 requires immediate intervention.
