### Safe Bed Positioning

**Fall Prevention**:
- âœ“ Bed in **lowest position** when patient in bed
- âœ“ Wheels **locked** at all times (unless moving)
- âœ“ Side rails per policy (usually 2-3, NOT all 4)

**When to Raise Bed**:
- During nursing care (proper ergonomics)
- **Always lower back down when finished**

**Trendelenburg** (head down):
- Shock management
- Postural drainage
- NEVER for CPR (use flat/supine)

**Reverse Trendelenburg** (feet down):
- GERD, aspiration prevention
- Improve breathing
- Reduce peripheral edema

### Common IV Pump Alarms

| Alarm | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **AIR IN LINE** | Air detected in tubing | Stop pump, check tubing for air, remove air, resume |
| **OCCLUSION** | Blockage in line | Check for kinked tubing, check IV site, assess for infiltration |
| **DOWNSTREAM** | Problem after pump | Check IV site, assess catheter patency |
| **UPSTREAM** | Problem before pump | Check IV bag/bottle, ensure not empty, check tubing |
| **LOW BATTERY** | Battery running low | Plug into outlet immediately |
| **DOOR OPEN** | Pump door not closed | Close pump door securely |

**NEVER**: Ignore or silence alarms repeatedly - investigate cause!

### Oxygen Delivery Devices

| Device | Flow Rate | FiO2 | When to Use |
|--------|-----------|------|-------------|
| **Nasal Cannula** | 1-6 L/min | 24-44% | Stable patient, mild hypoxia |

## ğŸ“ Practice Questions

<details>
<summary>Question 1: IV pump alarming "AIR IN LINE." What should nurse do first?</summary>

**Answer**: Stop the pump and check tubing for air

**Rationale**: Stop infusion immediately when air alarm sounds. Inspect tubing for air bubbles. Remove air by tapping tubing or aspirating. Never ignore IV pump alarms.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 2: Which blood pressure cuff should be used for an obese patient?</summary>

**Answer**: Large adult cuff (thigh cuff)

**Rationale**: Cuff bladder width should be 40% of arm circumference. Too small cuff gives falsely HIGH readings. Use appropriately sized cuff for accurate reading.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 3: Patient with COPD receiving oxygen via nasal cannula at 2 L/min. Patient requests oxygen be increased. What should nurse do?</summary>

**Answer**: Explain that oxygen requires physician order and assess patient's oxygen saturation

**Rationale**: Oxygen is a medication requiring physician order. Assess patient first (SpO2, respiratory status). High-flow oxygen can suppress respiratory drive in COPD patients. Notify physician of patient request.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 4: Before providing care at bedside, what should the nurse check regarding the bed?</summary>

**Answer**: Ensure bed wheels are locked

**Rationale**: Locked wheels prevent bed from moving during care, preventing falls/injuries. Check every time before providing care. Unlock only when intentionally moving bed.
</details>
