## ü¶† Transmission-Based Precautions

### Three Types

**Use IN ADDITION to Standard Precautions**

1. **Contact Precautions** - Direct/indirect contact transmission
2. **Droplet Precautions** - Large particle droplets (>5 microns)
3. **Airborne Precautions** - Small particles (<5 microns)

### Airborne Precautions PPE & Procedures

```
üö™ ROOM - AIRBORNE INFECTION ISOLATION ROOM (AIIR):
   ‚úì Private room REQUIRED
   ‚úì Negative pressure (air flows INTO room)
   ‚úì Air exhausted outside or HEPA filtered
   ‚úì Door must remain CLOSED
   ‚úì 6-12 air changes per hour minimum

üò∑ N95 RESPIRATOR MASK:
   ‚úì FIT-TESTED N95 respirator REQUIRED
   ‚úì Don BEFORE entering room
   ‚úì Seal-check each time
   ‚úì NOT a regular surgical mask
   ‚úì Remove after leaving room, close door

üß§ GLOVES/GOWN:
   ‚úì Per Standard Precautions
   ‚úì If contact with body fluids expected

üë§ PATIENT TRANSPORT:
   ‚úì Limit transport (essential only)
   ‚úì Patient wears surgical mask
   ‚úì Notify receiving department in advance

üö´ STAFF RESTRICTIONS:
   ‚úì Susceptible HCWs should NOT enter room with:
      - Measles (unless immune)
      - Varicella (unless immune/vaccinated)
```

### Special Considerations

**Tuberculosis (TB)**:
- Airborne + Contact if draining lesions
- Continue precautions until 3 consecutive negative sputum samples
- Directly Observed Therapy (DOT)

**Chickenpox/Shingles**:
- Disseminated = Airborne + Contact
- Localized shingles (can cover) = Contact only
- Immune staff can provide care

## üìè Order of Donning & Doffing PPE

### Donning PPE (Putting ON)

```
ORDER: "GMAN"
  1. GOWN (ties in back)
  2. MASK or N95 respirator
  3. GOGGLES/Face shield
  4. GLOVES (over gown cuffs)
```

### Doffing PPE (Taking OFF)

```
ORDER: "GGMG" (Most contaminated first)
  1. GLOVES (peel off, don't snap)
  2. GOGGLES/Face shield
  3. GOWN (roll inside-out)
  4. MASK/Respirator (touch ties only)

  THEN: Perform hand hygiene!
```

**Critical**: Remove gloves FIRST (most contaminated), mask LAST (don't contaminate face)

## üö™ Patient Placement Priority

### Private Room Assignment Priority

**When limited private rooms, prioritize**:

1. **Airborne precautions** (MUST have negative pressure room)
2. **Excessive coughing/secretions**
3. **Uncontrolled bodily fluids**
4. **Confused/non-compliant** (can't follow precautions)
5. **Immunocompromised** (protect from others)

### Cohorting

**Can room together if**:
- Same infection/pathogen
- Both on same precautions
- No other infections present

**Cannot room together**:
- Different infections
- Immunocompromised patient
- High-risk patient (wounds, devices)

## üßº Hand Hygiene - The Foundation

### When to Perform Hand Hygiene

**WHO's 5 Moments**:
1. **Before** patient contact
2. **Before** clean/aseptic procedure
3. **After** body fluid exposure risk
4. **After** patient contact
5. **After** contact with patient surroundings

### Soap & Water vs. Alcohol-Based Hand Rub

| Use SOAP & WATER When: | Alcohol Rub OK For: |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Hands visibly soiled | Hands not visibly soiled |
| **C. difficile** | MRSA, VRE |
| **Norovirus** | Most situations |
| After using restroom | Quick decontamination |
| Before eating | Between patient contacts |

**Technique**:
- Alcohol rub: 20 seconds (until dry)
- Soap & water: 20 seconds (sing Happy Birthday twice)

---

### Common NCLEX Questions

**Question Type 1**: "Which PPE is required?"
- Identify the precaution type first
- Then recall required PPE

**Question Type 2**: "Which patients can room together?"
- Same infection + same precaution = Yes
- Different infections = No
- One immunocompromised = No

**Question Type 3**: "Which room assignment?"
- Airborne MUST have AIIR
- Prioritize by risk/contamination level

## üìù Practice Questions

<details>
<summary>Question 1: Patient with pulmonary tuberculosis. What PPE is required?</summary>

**Answer**: N95 respirator mask, gown and gloves per standard precautions

**Rationale**: TB = Airborne precautions. Requires fit-tested N95 respirator (not surgical mask), negative pressure room, door closed. Gown/gloves only if contact with body fluids expected.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 2: Nurse caring for patient with C. difficile. What is most important for hand hygiene?</summary>

**Answer**: Wash hands with soap and water

**Rationale**: C. difficile spores are NOT killed by alcohol-based hand rubs. Must use soap and water. This is a CONTACT precaution organism requiring gown and gloves.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 3: Four patients need room assignment. Who should get the negative pressure room?</summary>

Patients:
A) MRSA wound infection
B) Influenza
C) Pulmonary tuberculosis
D) C. difficile diarrhea

**Answer: C** - Pulmonary tuberculosis

**Rationale**: TB requires AIRBORNE precautions with negative pressure room (AIIR). A = Contact, B = Droplet, D = Contact. Only airborne precautions REQUIRE negative pressure room.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 4: Which patients can be placed in the same room?</summary>

**Answer**: Two patients with influenza

**Rationale**: Cohorting allowed when patients have SAME infection and same precaution type. Cannot room different infections together or immunocompromised patients.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 5: Patient with measles. What precautions are required?</summary>

**Answer**: Airborne precautions - N95 mask, negative pressure room, door closed

**Rationale**: Measles = Airborne (remember MTV: Measles, TB, Varicella). Requires N95, AIIR room, closed door. Highly contagious. Only immune healthcare workers should provide care.
</details>
