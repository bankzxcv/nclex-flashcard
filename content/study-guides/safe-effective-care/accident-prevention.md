## üö® Fall Prevention

### Fall Risk Factors

**Intrinsic (Patient) Factors**:
```
HIGH RISK FOR FALLS:
‚úì Age >65 years
‚úì History of falls
‚úì Impaired mobility/gait instability
‚úì Weakness, decreased muscle strength
‚úì Impaired cognition/confusion/dementia
‚úì Urinary frequency/urgency/incontinence
‚úì Vision impairment
‚úì Orthostatic hypotension
‚úì Medications (sedatives, diuretics, antihypertensives)
‚úì Acute illness
‚úì Pain
‚úì Depression
```

**Extrinsic (Environmental) Factors**:
- Wet floors
- Poor lighting
- Clutter, obstacles
- Lack of handrails
- Improper footwear
- Bed/chair at wrong height
- Lack of assistive devices

---

### Fall Risk Assessment Tools

**Common Tools**:
- **Morse Fall Scale** (most common in hospitals)
- **Hendrich II Fall Risk Model**
- **STRATIFY**
- **Schmid Fall Risk Assessment Tool** (FRAT)

**When to Assess**:
- On admission
- With change in condition
- After a fall
- With medication changes
- Per facility policy (often every shift)

---

### Fall Prevention Interventions

```
UNIVERSAL FALL PRECAUTIONS (ALL PATIENTS):

BED SAFETY:
  ‚úì Bed in lowest position
  ‚úì Wheels locked
  ‚úì Side rails up (per policy - not all 4)

ENVIRONMENT:
  ‚úì Call light within reach
  ‚úì Personal items within reach
  ‚úì Clear pathways, remove clutter
  ‚úì Adequate lighting
  ‚úì Non-skid footwear/socks
  ‚úì Clean up spills immediately

TOILETING:
  ‚úì Scheduled toileting (q2h)
  ‚úì Bedside commode if needed
  ‚úì Assist to bathroom

MONITORING:
  ‚úì Frequent rounding (hourly rounding)
  ‚úì Observe for confusion, agitation
  ‚úì Check for orthostatic hypotension

EDUCATION:
  ‚úì Teach patient to call for help
  ‚úì Explain fall risks
  ‚úì Family education
```

---

### High-Risk Fall Interventions

**For patients at HIGH RISK**:
- ‚úì **Yellow** fall risk band/sign
- ‚úì Bed/chair alarms
- ‚úì Room close to nurse's station
- ‚úì Hourly rounding (or more frequent)
- ‚úì Mobility aids (walker, cane)
- ‚úì Physical therapy consult
- ‚úì Review medications (reduce sedatives)
- ‚úì Consider bed mat/low bed

**Controversial/AVOID**:
- ‚úó Restraints (increase fall risk and injury)
- ‚úó All 4 side rails up (considered restraint)

---

### Side Rails: Safety vs. Restraint

| 2-3 Side Rails | All 4 Side Rails Up |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Safety device | Considered restraint |
| Helps with position changes | Patient may climb over ‚Üí higher fall risk |
| Legal | Requires restraint order |
| Promotes independence | Restricts freedom |

**NCLEX Key**: All 4 side rails = Restraint

---

### Post-Fall Protocol

**Immediate Actions**:
1. **Stay with patient**, call for help
2. **Assess** for injury (don't move if injury suspected)
3. **Vital signs**, neurological assessment
4. **Notify** physician
5. **Complete** incident report
6. **Reassess** fall risk
7. **Implement** additional interventions

**Documentation**:
- Document in medical record factually
- "Patient found on floor" (not "patient fell")
- Describe patient condition, interventions
- Complete separate incident/occurrence report

## üíä Medication Error Prevention

### High-Alert Medications

**Medications with Highest Risk of Harm**:
```
HIGH-ALERT MEDICATIONS:
  ‚Ä¢ Insulin
  ‚Ä¢ Heparin, Warfarin
  ‚Ä¢ Opioids (morphine, fentanyl)
  ‚Ä¢ Sedatives
  ‚Ä¢ Chemotherapy
  ‚Ä¢ Potassium chloride IV
  ‚Ä¢ Neuromuscular blocking agents
  ‚Ä¢ Contrast media

REMEMBER: "I Have One Serious Concern - Patients Need Care"
(Insulin, Heparin, Opioids, Sedatives, Chemo, Potassium,
 Neuromuscular blockers, Contrast)
```

---

### Six Rights of Medication Administration

```
THE 6 RIGHTS (Minimum):

1. ‚úì RIGHT PATIENT
   ‚Ä¢ Two patient identifiers (name + DOB or MRN)
   ‚Ä¢ Check ID band
   ‚Ä¢ Ask patient to state name and DOB

2. ‚úì RIGHT MEDICATION
   ‚Ä¢ Check order
   ‚Ä¢ Verify medication name
   ‚Ä¢ Check label 3 times

3. ‚úì RIGHT DOSE
   ‚Ä¢ Verify dosage calculation
   ‚Ä¢ Double-check high-risk meds
   ‚Ä¢ Use calculator if needed

4. ‚úì RIGHT ROUTE
   ‚Ä¢ PO, IV, IM, SubQ, topical, etc.
   ‚Ä¢ Verify route is appropriate

5. ‚úì RIGHT TIME
   ‚Ä¢ Follow medication schedule
   ‚Ä¢ Know critical timing (antibiotics, insulin)

6. ‚úì RIGHT DOCUMENTATION
   ‚Ä¢ Document immediately after giving
   ‚Ä¢ Never document before giving
   ‚Ä¢ Include time, dose, route, site
```

**Additional Rights** (expanded):
- Right to REFUSE
- Right REASON (indication)
- Right RESPONSE (evaluate effectiveness)
- Right patient EDUCATION

---

### Triple Check Method

**Check medication label 3 times**:
1. When removing from storage
2. When preparing medication
3. Before administering to patient (at bedside)

---

### Strategies to Prevent Medication Errors

```
ERROR PREVENTION STRATEGIES:

BEFORE GIVING:
  ‚úì Minimize interruptions during med prep
  ‚úì Never leave medications unattended
  ‚úì Don't give medications prepared by others
  ‚úì Question orders that seem unusual
  ‚úì Use two-nurse verification for high-risk meds
  ‚úì Barcode scanning

CALCULATIONS:
  ‚úì Double-check all calculations
  ‚úì Have another nurse verify high-risk doses
  ‚úì Use calculator
  ‚úì Know usual dose ranges

LOOK-ALIKE/SOUND-ALIKE:
  ‚úì Be aware (e.g., hydrOXYzine vs hydrALAzine)
  ‚úì Use tall-man lettering
  ‚úì Read labels carefully
  ‚úì Don't rely on memory

IF UNSURE:
  ‚úì Look it up - never guess
  ‚úì Ask pharmacist
  ‚úì Verify with prescriber
  ‚úì Use drug reference
```

---

### When Medication Error Occurs

**Immediate Actions**:
1. **Assess patient** - vital signs, symptoms
2. **Notify physician** immediately
3. **Follow physician orders** for interventions
4. **Document** factually in medical record
5. **Complete** incident/error report
6. **Monitor** patient closely
7. **Be honest** with patient/family

**Documentation**:
- Medical record: Factual, objective
- "Patient received [medication] [dose] at [time]"
- Describe patient response, interventions
- Do NOT write "error" or "incident report filed" in medical record

**Incident Report**:
- Separate confidential document
- Detailed description of error
- Contributing factors
- Used for quality improvement
- NOT part of medical record

**Acceptable Identifiers**:
- ‚úì Name (full name)
- ‚úì Date of birth
- ‚úì Medical record number (MRN)

**NOT Acceptable**:

- ‚úó Rely on name card on door/bed

## üî™ Sharps Safety

### Needle Safety

```
SHARPS SAFETY RULES:

‚úì Never recap needles (most common cause of needlesticks)
‚úì Dispose immediately in sharps container
‚úì Don't overfill sharps containers (3/4 full maximum)
‚úì Carry sharps container to point of use
‚úì Never pass sharps hand-to-hand
‚úì Use safety-engineered devices (retractable needles)
‚úì Activate safety mechanism before disposal
‚úì Don't reach into sharps container
```

### If Needlestick Injury Occurs

**Immediate Actions**:
1. **Wash** with soap and water (don't squeeze)
2. **Report** to occupational health immediately
3. **Source patient testing** (HIV, Hepatitis B/C)
4. **Baseline testing** for exposed healthcare worker
5. **Post-exposure prophylaxis** if indicated
6. **Follow-up** testing at intervals
7. **Complete** incident report

**Time is critical**: HIV prophylaxis most effective within 2 hours

## üö´ Restraint Use & Alternatives

### Definition of Restraint

**Restraint**: Any manual method, physical or mechanical device, material, or equipment that immobilizes or reduces ability to move freely

**Types**:
- Physical: Wrist/ankle restraints, vests, mitts
- Chemical: Sedating medications for behavior control
- Seclusion: Involuntary confinement
- Environmental: All 4 side rails up

---

### When Restraints May Be Used

**ONLY when**:
- Patient is immediate danger to self or others
- Less restrictive alternatives have failed
- Ordered by physician
- Discontinued as soon as possible

**NEVER use restraints for**:
- ‚úó Convenience
- ‚úó Punishment
- ‚úó Staffing shortages
- ‚úó Staff convenience

---

### Alternatives to Restraints (Try FIRST)

```
RESTRAINT ALTERNATIVES:

REORIENTATION:
  ‚úì Frequent reorientation
  ‚úì Reality orientation
  ‚úì Clocks, calendars, family photos

ENVIRONMENT:
  ‚úì Move closer to nurse's station
  ‚úì Reduce stimulation
  ‚úì Adequate lighting
  ‚úì Familiar objects from home

ACTIVITIES:
  ‚úì Diversional activities
  ‚úì Music therapy
  ‚úì Allow pacing in safe area

TOILETING:
  ‚úì Scheduled toileting
  ‚úì Bedside commode

PHYSICAL NEEDS:
  ‚úì Pain management
  ‚úì Positioning for comfort
  ‚úì Treat underlying cause

FAMILY:
  ‚úì Family presence
  ‚úì Sitters/companions
  ‚úì Volunteers

MEDICATION REVIEW:
  ‚úì Reduce sedatives if possible
  ‚úì Treat delirium causes
```

---

### Restraint Protocol (If Must Use)

**Requirements**:
- ‚úì Physician order (specific type, duration)
- ‚úì Time-limited (usually 4 hours adult, 2 hours ages 9-17, 1 hour <9 years)
- ‚úì Must be renewed
- ‚úì Face-to-face assessment by physician within 1 hour

**Monitoring**:
- ‚úì Check every 15-30 minutes
- ‚úì Assess circulation, skin integrity
- ‚úì Release and reposition every 2 hours
- ‚úì Offer toileting, fluids, nutrition
- ‚úì ROM exercises
- ‚úì Document all assessments

**Application**:
- ‚úì Apply properly (not too tight)
- ‚úì Tie to bed frame (not side rails)
- ‚úì Quick-release knots
- ‚úì Ability to access quickly in emergency

## üî• Fire Safety

### Fire Response: RACE

```
R - RESCUE patients in immediate danger
A - ALARM - activate fire alarm
C - CONTAIN - close doors, turn off oxygen
E - EXTINGUISH or Evacuate
```

**Priority**: RESCUE always first!

---

### Fire Extinguisher Use: PASS

```
P - PULL the pin
A - AIM at base of fire
S - SQUEEZE handle
S - SWEEP side to side
```

---

### Oxygen Safety

**Oxygen is combustible** - increases fire risk

```
OXYGEN SAFETY RULES:

‚úì "No Smoking" signs posted
‚úì No open flames (candles, lighters)
‚úì No electrical equipment that sparks
‚úì Avoid oil-based products near oxygen
‚úì Secure oxygen tanks upright
‚úì Turn off oxygen if fire alarm
‚úì Use cotton blankets (not wool/synthetic)
```

## üéØ NCLEX Key Concepts

### Mnemonic: **SAFE CARE**

- **S**ide rails (2-3, not all 4)
- **A**larms (bed/chair alarms for high risk)
- **F**all risk assessment on admission
- **E**nvironment (clutter-free, adequate light)
- **C**all light within reach
- **A**ssist with toileting (q2h)
- **R**ound frequently (hourly)
- **E**ducate patient and family

---

### Common NCLEX Scenarios

**Fall Prevention**:
- Use interventions BEFORE restraints
- All 4 side rails = Restraint
- Bed in lowest position

**Medication Errors**:
- Never give meds prepared by someone else
- Two patient identifiers required
- Question unusual orders
- Notify physician immediately if error

**Restraints**:
- Use only as last resort
- Alternatives tried first
- Physician order required
- Frequent monitoring required

## üìù Practice Questions

<details>
<summary>Question 1: Patient trying to get out of bed, pulling at IV. What should nurse do FIRST?</summary>

**Answer**: Stay with patient and ask what they need

**Rationale**: Try alternatives first before considering restraints. Patient may need toileting, pain relief, reorientation. Assess needs first. Restraints are last resort.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 2: Nurse gives patient wrong medication. What is the FIRST action?</summary>

**Answer**: Assess patient's vital signs and condition

**Rationale**: Patient safety first. Assess for adverse effects. Then notify physician, follow orders, monitor patient. Document factually. Complete incident report separately.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 3: Which is a restraint?</summary>

**Answer**: Raising all four side rails on patient's bed

**Rationale**: All 4 side rails up is considered restraint - restricts freedom of movement and patient may climb over (increased fall risk). Use only 2-3 side rails.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Question 4: Fire alarm sounds. What is nurse's FIRST action?</summary>

**Answer**: Rescue patients in immediate danger

**Rationale**: RACE - Rescue first! Move patients away from fire/smoke. Then Alarm (if not already activated), Contain (close doors), Extinguish or Evacuate.
</details>
