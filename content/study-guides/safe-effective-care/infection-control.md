# Infection Control & Asepsis

## Overview
Infection prevention and control is a critical nursing responsibility and heavily tested on the NCLEX. Understanding the chain of infection, aseptic techniques, and isolation precautions is essential for safe patient care.

Breaking any link in the chain stops infection transmission:

```
1. Infectious Agent â†’ 2. Reservoir â†’ 3. Portal of Exit â†’
4. Mode of Transmission â†’ 5. Portal of Entry â†’ 6. Susceptible Host
```

### 1. Infectious Agent
- **Bacteria**: Staphylococcus, E. coli, C. diff
- **Viruses**: Influenza, HIV, Hepatitis
- **Fungi**: Candida, Aspergillus
- **Parasites**: Malaria, Giardia

### 2. Reservoir (Where pathogen lives)
- Humans, animals, environment
- **Nursing intervention**: Proper waste disposal, environmental cleaning

### 3. Portal of Exit
- Respiratory tract (coughing, sneezing)
- GI tract (feces, vomitus)
- Blood/body fluids
- Skin/mucous membranes
- **Nursing intervention**: Cover nose/mouth when coughing, proper wound care

### 4. Mode of Transmission
- **Contact** (direct or indirect)
- **Droplet** (within 3 feet)
- **Airborne** (suspended in air)
- **Vector** (insects, animals)
- **Nursing intervention**: Hand hygiene, PPE, isolation precautions

### 5. Portal of Entry
- Same routes as exit
- **Nursing intervention**: Aseptic technique, intact skin, sterile procedures

### 6. Susceptible Host
- Immunocompromised, very young/old, chronic illness
- **Nursing intervention**: Nutrition, immunizations, avoid unnecessary invasive procedures

## ðŸ§¼ Hand Hygiene

### The MOST Important Infection Control Measure

**When to perform hand hygiene**:
- Before and after patient contact
- Before aseptic procedures
- After body fluid exposure risk
- After touching patient surroundings
- Before eating
- After using restroom

### Soap and Water vs. Alcohol-Based Hand Rub

| Use Soap & Water When: | Alcohol-Based Rub OK When: |
|------------------------|---------------------------|
| Hands visibly soiled | Hands not visibly dirty |
| **C. difficile** exposure | Routine patient care |
| Before eating | Between patients |
| After restroom | After removing gloves |
| **Spore-forming organisms** | Most situations |

**Important**: Alcohol does NOT kill C. diff spores or norovirus. Must use soap and water!

### Proper Handwashing Technique
1. Wet hands with warm water
2. Apply soap
3. Lather for **at least 20 seconds** (sing "Happy Birthday" twice)
4. Scrub all surfaces, between fingers, under nails
5. Rinse thoroughly
6. Dry with paper towel
7. Use paper towel to turn off faucet

### Apply to ALL Patients, ALL Times

Assume all blood and body fluids are potentially infectious.

### Body Fluids Requiring Precautions
- Blood
- All body fluids, secretions, excretions (except sweat)
- Non-intact skin
- Mucous membranes

### PPE Donning (Putting On) Order
1. **Gown** (ties in back)
2. **Mask/Respirator** (secure ties/elastic)
3. **Goggles/Face Shield** (adjust to fit)
4. **Gloves** (cover gown cuffs)

### PPE Doffing (Removing) Order
**Most contaminated items removed first!**

1. **Gloves** (peel off, inside out)
2. **Goggles/Face Shield** (handle by headband only)
3. **Gown** (unfasten, roll inside out)
4. **Mask** (remove by ties only, don't touch front)
5. **HAND HYGIENE** immediately after removal

## ðŸšª Transmission-Based Precautions

Use in ADDITION to Standard Precautions for known or suspected infections.

### Contact Precautions

**When**: Direct or indirect contact transmission

**Infections requiring Contact Precautions**:
- **MRSA** (Methicillin-Resistant Staph Aureus)
- **VRE** (Vancomycin-Resistant Enterococcus)
- **C. difficile**
- **Scabies**, **Lice**
- **RSV** (Respiratory Syncytial Virus)
- **Herpes Simplex** (disseminated)
- **Wound infections** with drainage

**PPE Required**:
- Gloves upon entry
- Gown if contact with patient/environment anticipated
- Private room preferred (or cohort with same infection)
- Dedicated equipment (stethoscope, BP cuff, thermometer)

**Mnemonic - MRS. WEE**:
- **M**RSA
- **R**SV
- **S**cabies
- **W**ounds (infected, draining)
- **E**nteric infections (C. diff)
- **E**ye infections (conjunctivitis)

### Droplet Precautions

**When**: Large particle droplets (>5 microns) transmitted through coughing, sneezing, talking

**Range**: Within **3 feet** of patient

**Infections requiring Droplet Precautions**:
- **Influenza**
- **Pertussis** (Whooping Cough)
- **Meningitis** (bacterial)
- **Pneumonia** (bacterial)
- **Mumps**
- **Rubella**
- **Diphtheria** (pharyngeal)
- **Streptococcal pharyngitis**
- **Scarlet Fever**

**PPE Required**:
- **Surgical mask** upon entry (within 3 feet)
- Private room preferred (or cohort)
- Can share room if beds >3 feet apart

**Mnemonic - SPIDERMAN**:
- **S**trep throat / **S**carlet fever
- **P**ertussis / **P**neumonia
- **I**nfluenza
- **D**iphtheria
- **E**piglottitis (bacterial)
- **R**ubella
- **M**umps / **M**eningitis
- **A**denovirus
- **N**/A

### Airborne Precautions

**When**: Small particle aerosols (<5 microns) remain suspended in air for long periods

**Infections requiring Airborne Precautions**:
- **Tuberculosis** (TB)
- **Measles** (Rubeola)
- **Chickenpox** (Varicella)
- **Disseminated Herpes Zoster** (Shingles)

**PPE Required**:
- **N95 respirator** (fit-tested) before entry
- **Negative pressure room** (air flows IN, not out)
- Door must remain closed
- Limit transport; patient wears surgical mask if must leave room

**Mnemonic - MTV**:
- **M**easles (Rubeola)
- **T**uberculosis
- **V**aricella (Chickenpox) / Disseminated Zoster

**Special Note**: Visitors/staff without immunity should NOT enter measles/varicella rooms

## ðŸ§ª Aseptic Technique

### Medical Asepsis ("Clean Technique")
- Reduces number of microorganisms
- Prevents transmission
- Used in most patient care activities
- Examples: Hand hygiene, clean gloves, clean equipment

### Surgical Asepsis ("Sterile Technique")
- **Eliminates ALL** microorganisms
- Used for invasive procedures
- Examples: Surgery, catheter insertion, wound care

### Sterile Field Principles

**A sterile object remains sterile only when touched by another sterile object**

Rules:
1. **Sterile touches sterile** = Sterile
2. **Sterile touches unsterile** = Contaminated
3. **Edge (1 inch) of sterile field** = Unsterile
4. **Hold sterile items above waist** level
5. **Never turn back** on sterile field
6. **Never reach across** sterile field
7. **Wet sterile field** = Contaminated (strike-through)
8. **Doubt sterility?** = Consider contaminated

### Opening Sterile Packages
- Open **flap away** from you first
- Open **side flaps** next (don't reach across)
- Open **final flap toward** you last

## ðŸ’‰ Preventing Healthcare-Associated Infections (HAIs)

### Catheter-Associated UTI (CAUTI)
**Prevention**:
- Insert only when necessary
- Maintain closed drainage system
- Keep bag below bladder level
- Daily perineal care
- Remove as soon as possible

### Central Line-Associated Bloodstream Infection (CLABSI)
**Prevention**:
- Strict sterile technique during insertion
- Daily chlorhexidine bath
- Assess need daily
- Change dressing per protocol
- Scrub hub before accessing line

### Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP)
**Prevention**:
- Elevate head of bed 30-45Â°
- Oral care with chlorhexidine every 2 hours
- Suction subglottic secretions
- Daily sedation vacation
- Assess readiness to wean daily

### Surgical Site Infection (SSI)
**Prevention**:
- Preoperative antibiotics (within 1 hour of incision)
- Clipper (not razor) for hair removal
- Maintain normothermia
- Glucose control
- Sterile technique during dressing changes

## ðŸ©º NCLEX Tips

### High-Yield Testing Points

1. **Hand hygiene** is always the answer for "best way to prevent infection"
2. **N95 for airborne** (TB, measles, varicella), **surgical mask for droplet**
3. **C. diff requires soap and water**, not alcohol-based hand rub
4. **Remove most contaminated PPE first** (gloves)
5. **Private room** required for airborne precautions
6. **Negative pressure room** for airborne, **positive pressure** for neutropenic patients
7. When in doubt about isolation type, **choose the most protective**

### Common NCLEX Scenarios

**Scenario**: Which patient can share a room?
- **Correct**: Two patients with SAME infection
- **Incorrect**: Different infections, immunocompromised with any infection

**Scenario**: Nurse forgot to perform hand hygiene. What is priority?
- **Answer**: Perform hand hygiene immediately

**Scenario**: Which task can be delegated to UAP?
- **Can delegate**: Vital signs, bathing (with proper PPE training)
- **Cannot delegate**: Assessment, sterile procedures, patient teaching
