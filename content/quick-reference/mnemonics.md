## Respiratory

### TRAPP - Pneumothorax Signs
- **T**achypnea
- **R**educed breath sounds
- **A**symmetric chest expansion
- **P**ain (sudden, sharp)
- **P**ressure (subcutaneous emphysema)

### ARDS - Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome
- **A**bnormal chest X-ray (bilateral infiltrates)
- **R**efractory hypoxemia (doesn't respond to O2)
- **D**yspnea (severe)
- **S**tiff lungs (decreased compliance)

### COUGH - Respiratory Assessment
- **C**olor of sputum
- **O**nset
- **U**nderstanding triggers
- **G**reen/yellow (infection)
- **H**emoptysis

### COPD Meds - BS
- **B**ronchodilators (albuterol)

## Neurological

### FAST - Stroke Assessment

## Endocrine

### DKA vs HHS - Hyperglycemia
**DKA (Type 1):**
- **D**ehydration
- **K**ussmaul breathing
- **A**cidosis (pH <7.3)

**Remember:** DKA has ketones; HHS does not

### HOT & DRY - Hyperglycemia
- **Hot** and dry skin
- Sugar **high**

### COLD & CLAMMY - Hypoglycemia
- **Cold** and clammy skin
- Need some **candy**

### TIRED - Hypothyroidism
- **T**ired/fatigue
- **I**ntolerance to cold
- **R**ate decreased (HR, metabolism)
- **E**dema (myxedema)
- **D**ry skin, hair loss

### SWEATY - Hyperthyroidism
- **S**weating
- **W**eight loss
- **E**ye problems (exophthalmos)
- **A**nxious/irritable
- **T**achycardia
- **Y**oung appearance (increased metabolism)

### Cushing's Syndrome - BUFFALO
- **B**uffalo hump
- **U**lcer/gastric issues
- **F**at moon face
- **F**at truncal obesity
- **A**menorrhea
- **L**ow potassium
- **O**steoporosis

- **A**V block
- **B**radycardia

## Pediatrics

### APGAR Score Components
- **A**ppearance (color)
- **P**ulse (heart rate)
- **G**rimace (reflex irritability)
- **A**ctivity (muscle tone)
- **R**espiration

**Score 0-10 at 1 min and 5 min**
- 7-10 = Normal
- 4-6 = Moderate distress
- 0-3 = Severe distress

### CROUP - Assessment
- **C**ough (barking seal)
- **R**espiratory stridor
- **O**bstruction (upper airway)
- **U**nder 3 years old
- **P**rogressively worse at night

### EPIGLOTTITIS - 4 D's
- **D**ysphagia (difficulty swallowing)
- **D**rooling
- **D**ystonic positioning (tripod)
- **D**istressed respiratory effort

**DON'T:** Examine throat, lay patient flat, leave patient alone

### Kawasaki Disease - CRASH & BURN
- **C**onjunctivitis (bilateral, non-purulent)
- **R**ash (polymorphous)
- **A**denopathy (cervical lymph nodes)
- **S**trawberry tongue
- **H**and/foot erythema & edema
- **BURN** = Fever >5 days

## Burns

### Rule of Nines (Adult)
- **H**ead: 9%
- **A**rm (each): 9%
- **C**hest (front): 18%
- **B**ack: 18%
- **L**eg (each): 18%
- **P**erineum: 1%

### Burn Depth - 1st, 2nd, 3rd
- **1st degree**: Superficial, painful, red, no blisters (sunburn)
- **2nd degree**: Partial thickness, very painful, blisters
- **3rd degree**: Full thickness, painless (nerve damage), white/black

### Parkland Formula - Burn Fluid Resuscitation
**4 mL × kg × % TBSA burned**
- Give 1/2 in first 8 hours
- Give 1/2 in next 16 hours

## Lab Values

### MODS - Monitor in DKA/HHS
- **M**onitor glucose

### Potassium Imbalance Signs
**HYPERkalemia → MURDER**
- **M**uscle weakness
- **U**rine output decreased
- **R**espiratory distress
- **D**ecreased cardiac contractility
- **E**CG changes (tall peaked T waves)
- **R**hythm changes (bradycardia)

**HYPOkalemia → SUCTION**
- **S**keletal muscle weakness
- **U**-wave on ECG
- **C**onstipation
- **T**oxicity from digoxin
- **I**rregular/weak pulse
- **O**rthostatic hypotension
- **N**ausea/vomiting

### Calcium Imbalance - CATS
**HYPERcalcemia:**
- **C**onstipation
- **A**rrhythmias
- **T**hirsty
- **S**tones (kidney)

**"Stones, bones, groans, psychiatric overtones"**

**HYPOcalcemia → CATS**
- **C**onvulsions
- **A**rrhythmias
- **T**etany (Chvostek's, Trousseau's signs)
- **S**pasm

### Trousseau's Sign
- Inflate BP cuff above systolic for 3 min
- Positive = hand/finger spasm
- Indicates hypocalcemia/hypomagnesemia

### Chvostek's Sign
- Tap facial nerve anterior to ear
- Positive = facial twitching
- Indicates hypocalcemia/hypomagnesemia

## Quick Memory Aids

### LEFT vs RIGHT
**"WHITE on RIGHT, SMOKE over FIRE"**
- Oxygen (white) on right side of flowmeter
- Air (yellow) on left
- Lithotomy position: Left leg first

### Trendelenburg - HEAD DOWN
**"TRENCH is DOWN"** - Head lower than feet

### Insulin - CLEAR before CLOUDY
**"Clear skies before a cloudy day"**
- Regular (clear) before NPH (cloudy)

### NG Tube Placement
**"If it's IN the LUNG, you'll hear NO sound"**
- Air insufflation over stomach = whoosh sound
- X-ray confirmation is gold standard
