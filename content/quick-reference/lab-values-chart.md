# Lab Values Quick Reference Chart

## Hematology

| Test | Normal Range | Critical Values | Notes |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| **RBC** | M: 4.7-6.1 million/mcL<br>F: 4.2-5.4 million/mcL | <2.5 or >6.5 | Low = anemia; High = polycythemia |
| **Hemoglobin (Hgb)** | M: 14-18 g/dL<br>F: 12-16 g/dL | <5 or >20 | Low = anemia; High = dehydration |
| **Hematocrit (Hct)** | M: 42-52%<br>F: 37-47% | <15% or >60% | 3x hemoglobin |
| **WBC** | 5,000-10,000/mm³ | <2,500 or >30,000 | Low = infection risk; High = infection/leukemia |
| **Platelets** | 150,000-400,000/mm³ | <20,000 or >1 million | Low = bleeding risk; High = clotting risk |
| **Neutrophils** | 55-70% (2,500-8,000/mm³) | <500 ANC | Severe risk if ANC <1,000 |
| **Lymphocytes** | 20-40% | - | High in viral infections |
| **Monocytes** | 2-8% | - | High in chronic infections |
| **Eosinophils** | 1-4% | - | High in allergies/parasites |
| **Basophils** | 0.5-1% | - | High in allergic reactions |

## Coagulation

| Test | Normal Range | Critical Values | Notes |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| **PT** | 11-12.5 seconds | >30 seconds | Monitors warfarin |
| **INR** | 0.8-1.1 | >5 | Therapeutic on warfarin: 2-3 |
| **aPTT** | 30-40 seconds | >70 seconds | Monitors heparin (1.5-2x normal) |
| **D-dimer** | <0.5 mcg/mL | - | Elevated in DVT/PE |

## Chemistry - Electrolytes

| Test | Normal Range | Critical Values | Notes |
|------|--------------|------------------|-------|
| **Sodium (Na)** | 136-145 mEq/L | <120 or >160 | Hypo = confusion; Hyper = thirst |
| **Potassium (K)** | 3.5-5.0 mEq/L | <2.5 or >6.5 | Most critical! Affects heart rhythm |
| **Chloride (Cl)** | 98-106 mEq/L | <80 or >115 | Follows sodium |
| **CO2/Bicarb** | 23-30 mEq/L | <10 or >40 | Acid-base balance |
| **Calcium (Ca)** | 9.0-10.5 mg/dL | <6 or >13 | Low = tetany; High = stones/bones |
| **Magnesium (Mg)** | 1.3-2.1 mEq/L | <1 or >4.9 | Low = seizures; High = decreased reflexes |
| **Phosphorus (P)** | 3.0-4.5 mg/dL | <1 or >8.9 | Inverse to calcium |

## Renal Function

| Test | Normal Range | Critical Values | Notes |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| **BUN** | 10-20 mg/dL | >100 | Elevated in dehydration/renal failure |
| **Creatinine** | 0.5-1.2 mg/dL | >4 | More specific for renal function |
| **BUN/Creat Ratio** | 10:1 to 20:1 | - | >20:1 = dehydration |
| **GFR** | >60 mL/min | <15 | <60 = chronic kidney disease |
| **Uric Acid** | M: 4.0-8.5 mg/dL<br>F: 2.7-7.3 mg/dL | - | Elevated in gout |

## Liver Function

| Test | Normal Range | Critical Values | Notes |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| **AST (SGOT)** | 0-35 U/L | >1,000 | Elevated in liver damage |
| **ALT (SGPT)** | 4-36 U/L | >1,000 | More specific for liver |
| **Alkaline Phos** | 30-120 U/L | - | Elevated in bone/liver disease |
| **Bilirubin Total** | 0.3-1.0 mg/dL | >12 | Elevated = jaundice |
| **Bilirubin Direct** | 0.1-0.3 mg/dL | - | Conjugated |
| **Albumin** | 3.5-5.0 g/dL | <2.0 | Low = malnutrition/liver disease |
| **Total Protein** | 6.4-8.3 g/dL | - | Albumin + globulin |
| **Ammonia** | 10-80 mcg/dL | >200 | Elevated in hepatic encephalopathy |

## Glucose & Metabolic

| Test | Normal Range | Critical Values | Notes |
|------|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Glucose (Fasting)** | 70-100 mg/dL | <40 or >400 | <70 = hypoglycemia; >126 = diabetes |
| **Glucose (Random)** | <125 mg/dL | <40 or >400 | >200 with symptoms = diabetes |
| **HbA1c** | <5.7% | - | 5.7-6.4% = prediabetes; >6.5% = diabetes |

## Cardiac Markers

| Test | Normal Range | Peak Time | Notes |
|------|--------------|-----------|-------|
| **Troponin I** | <0.04 ng/mL | 12-24 hrs | Most specific for MI |
| **Troponin T** | <0.01 ng/mL | 12-24 hrs | Most specific for MI |
| **CK-MB** | <3% total CK | 12-24 hrs | Cardiac muscle specific |
| **Myoglobin** | <90 mcg/L | 1-4 hrs | Early marker, not specific |
| **BNP** | <100 pg/mL | - | Elevated in heart failure |

## Lipid Panel

| Test | Normal Range | Risk Levels | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **Total Cholesterol** | <200 mg/dL | >240 = high risk | Desirable <200 |
| **LDL** | <100 mg/dL | >160 = high risk | "Bad" cholesterol |
| **HDL** | >40 mg/dL (M)<br>>50 mg/dL (F) | <40 = risk factor | "Good" cholesterol |
| **Triglycerides** | <150 mg/dL | >200 = high | Fasting required |

## Thyroid Function

| Test | Normal Range | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **TSH** | 0.4-4.0 mIU/L | High = hypothyroid; Low = hyperthyroid |
| **T4 (Total)** | 4-12 mcg/dL | High = hyperthyroid; Low = hypothyroid |
| **T3 (Total)** | 70-195 ng/dL | More sensitive than T4 |

## Arterial Blood Gases (ABG)

| Test | Normal Range | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **pH** | 7.35-7.45 | <7.35 = acidosis; >7.45 = alkalosis |
| **PaCO2** | 35-45 mmHg | Respiratory component |
| **HCO3** | 22-26 mEq/L | Metabolic component |
| **PaO2** | 80-100 mmHg | Oxygenation |
| **SaO2** | 95-100% | Oxygen saturation |

## Urinalysis

| Test | Normal | Abnormal Findings |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| **pH** | 4.6-8.0 | <4.6 acidosis; >8.0 infection |
| **Specific Gravity** | 1.005-1.030 | Low = overhydration; High = dehydration |
| **Protein** | Negative | Positive = kidney disease |
| **Glucose** | Negative | Positive = diabetes |
| **Ketones** | Negative | Positive = DKA, starvation |
| **Blood** | Negative | Positive = infection, stones, trauma |
| **Nitrites** | Negative | Positive = UTI |
| **Leukocytes** | Negative | Positive = infection |

## Quick Reference Notes

**Critical Lab Value Response:**
- Verify result with repeat test if unexpected
- Notify provider immediately
- Assess patient
- Document findings
- Prepare for interventions

**Panic Values = Immediate notification required**
