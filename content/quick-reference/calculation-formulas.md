# Dosage Calculation Formulas & Examples

## Basic Calculation Formula

### Universal Dosage Formula
```
Desired Dose
─────────── × Quantity = Amount to Give
Have on Hand
```

**OR**

```
What you WANT
─────────────── × What it's IN = What to GIVE
What you HAVE
```

## Medication Calculations

### Tablet/Capsule Calculations

**Formula:**
```
Desired (ordered)
───────────────── × Tablet quantity = Tablets to give
Available (on hand)
```

#### Example 1: Simple Calculation
- **Ordered:** 500 mg
- **Available:** 250 mg per tablet
- **Calculate:** 500 mg / 250 mg × 1 tablet = **2 tablets**

#### Example 2: Smaller Dose
- **Ordered:** 0.125 mg
- **Available:** 0.25 mg per tablet
- **Calculate:** 0.125 mg / 0.25 mg × 1 tablet = **0.5 tablet** (1/2 tablet)

#### Example 3: Conversion Needed
- **Ordered:** 0.5 g
- **Available:** 250 mg per tablet
- **Convert:** 0.5 g = 500 mg
- **Calculate:** 500 mg / 250 mg × 1 tablet = **2 tablets**

### Liquid Oral Medications

**Formula:** Same as tablet formula
```
Desired
─────── × Volume on hand = Volume to give
Have
```

#### Example 1: Basic Liquid
- **Ordered:** 400 mg
- **Available:** 250 mg per 5 mL
- **Calculate:** 400 mg / 250 mg × 5 mL = **8 mL**

#### Example 2: Pediatric Suspension
- **Ordered:** 75 mg
- **Available:** 125 mg per 5 mL
- **Calculate:** 75 mg / 125 mg × 5 mL = **3 mL**

#### Example 3: With Conversion
- **Ordered:** 1 g
- **Available:** 500 mg per 10 mL
- **Convert:** 1 g = 1,000 mg
- **Calculate:** 1,000 mg / 500 mg × 10 mL = **20 mL**

### Parenteral (Injectable) Medications

**Formula:** Same universal formula
```
Desired
─────── × Volume = mL to give
Have
```

#### Example 1: IM Injection
- **Ordered:** Morphine 8 mg IM
- **Available:** 10 mg per mL
- **Calculate:** 8 mg / 10 mg × 1 mL = **0.8 mL**

#### Example 2: SubQ Injection
- **Ordered:** Heparin 5,000 units SubQ
- **Available:** 10,000 units per mL
- **Calculate:** 5,000 units / 10,000 units × 1 mL = **0.5 mL**

#### Example 3: Insulin
- **Ordered:** 15 units Regular insulin
- **Available:** 100 units per mL (U-100)
- **Use:** Insulin syringe marked in units
- **Give:** **15 units** on insulin syringe

#### Example 4: Reconstitution
- **Ordered:** 500 mg IM
- **Available:** 1 g powder vial
- **Directions:** Add 2.5 mL sterile water = 3 mL total (1 g per 3 mL)
- **Convert:** 1 g = 1,000 mg; So 1,000 mg per 3 mL
- **Calculate:** 500 mg / 1,000 mg × 3 mL = **1.5 mL**

## IV Calculations

### IV Flow Rate (gtts/min) - Gravity Drip

**Formula:**
```
Total Volume (mL) × Drop Factor (gtts/mL)
─────────────────────────────────────── = gtts/min
Time (minutes)
```

**OR simplified:**
```
mL/hr × Drop Factor
─────────────────── = gtts/min
60 minutes
```

#### Drop Factors to Remember:
- **Macrodrip:** 10, 15, or 20 gtts/mL (standard)
- **Microdrip:** 60 gtts/mL (used for small volumes)

**Shortcut:** If microdrip (60 gtts/mL), gtts/min = mL/hr

#### Example 1: Standard IV
- **Ordered:** 1,000 mL D5W over 8 hours
- **Drop factor:** 15 gtts/mL
- **Calculate mL/hr:** 1,000 mL / 8 hr = 125 mL/hr
- **Calculate gtts/min:** (125 × 15) / 60 = **31 gtts/min**

#### Example 2: Microdrip
- **Ordered:** 500 mL NS over 10 hours
- **Drop factor:** 60 gtts/mL (microdrip)
- **Calculate mL/hr:** 500 mL / 10 hr = 50 mL/hr
- **Calculate gtts/min:** 50 mL/hr = **50 gtts/min** (with microdrip)

#### Example 3: Short Duration
- **Ordered:** 100 mL antibiotic over 30 minutes
- **Drop factor:** 10 gtts/mL
- **Calculate:** (100 mL × 10) / 30 min = **33 gtts/min**

### IV Flow Rate (mL/hr) - Pump

**Formula:**
```
Total Volume (mL)
───────────────── = mL/hr
Time (hours)
```

#### Example 1: Basic Infusion
- **Ordered:** 1,000 mL LR over 8 hours
- **Calculate:** 1,000 mL / 8 hr = **125 mL/hr**

#### Example 2: Shorter Duration
- **Ordered:** 250 mL over 2 hours
- **Calculate:** 250 mL / 2 hr = **125 mL/hr**

#### Example 3: Minutes to Hours
- **Ordered:** 50 mL over 30 minutes
- **Convert:** 30 min = 0.5 hr
- **Calculate:** 50 mL / 0.5 hr = **100 mL/hr**

### IV Medications - mcg/min to mL/hr

**Formula:**
```
Ordered dose (mcg/min) × 60 min/hr
────────────────────────────────────── = mL/hr
Concentration (mcg/mL)
```

#### Example 1: Dopamine
- **Ordered:** Dopamine 400 mcg/min
- **Available:** 400 mg in 250 mL D5W
- **Concentration:** 400 mg = 400,000 mcg in 250 mL = 1,600 mcg/mL
- **Calculate:** (400 mcg/min × 60) / 1,600 mcg/mL = **15 mL/hr**

#### Example 2: Nitroglycerin
- **Ordered:** 10 mcg/min
- **Available:** 50 mg in 250 mL D5W
- **Concentration:** 50 mg = 50,000 mcg in 250 mL = 200 mcg/mL
- **Calculate:** (10 mcg/min × 60) / 200 mcg/mL = **3 mL/hr**

### IV Medications - mcg/kg/min to mL/hr

**Formula:**
```
Ordered dose (mcg/kg/min) × Weight (kg) × 60 min/hr
───────────────────────────────────────────────────── = mL/hr
Concentration (mcg/mL)
```

#### Example 1: Dobutamine
- **Ordered:** 5 mcg/kg/min
- **Patient weight:** 70 kg
- **Available:** 500 mg in 250 mL D5W
- **Concentration:** 500 mg = 500,000 mcg in 250 mL = 2,000 mcg/mL
- **Calculate:** (5 × 70 × 60) / 2,000 = **10.5 mL/hr**

#### Example 2: Dopamine
- **Ordered:** 3 mcg/kg/min
- **Patient weight:** 80 kg
- **Available:** 400 mg in 250 mL D5W
- **Concentration:** 400,000 mcg / 250 mL = 1,600 mcg/mL
- **Calculate:** (3 × 80 × 60) / 1,600 = **9 mL/hr**

### IV Bolus/Push Medications

**Formula:**
```
Total dose to give
────────────────── = Time to push (minutes)
Safe rate per minute
```

#### Example 1: IV Push
- **Ordered:** Furosemide 40 mg IV push
- **Available:** 10 mg per mL (4 mL needed)
- **Safe rate:** No faster than 20 mg/min
- **Calculate:** 40 mg / 20 mg/min = **2 minutes minimum**

#### Example 2: Diluted IV Push
- **Ordered:** Morphine 4 mg IV push
- **Available:** 10 mg per mL
- **Volume:** 0.4 mL of morphine, dilute to 5 mL with NS
- **Safe rate:** Push over 4-5 minutes
- **Give:** **5 mL over 4-5 minutes** (approximately 1 mL per minute)

## Pediatric Calculations

### Weight Conversion - Pounds to Kilograms

**Formula:**
```
Pounds (lb)
─────────── = Kilograms (kg)
2.2
```

#### Examples:
- 44 lb / 2.2 = **20 kg**
- 110 lb / 2.2 = **50 kg**
- 15 lb / 2.2 = **6.8 kg**

### Safe Dose Range Calculation

**Steps:**
1. Convert weight to kg
2. Calculate safe dose range (multiply kg × low dose and kg × high dose)
3. Compare ordered dose to safe range

#### Example 1: Antibiotic
- **Ordered:** Amoxicillin 250 mg PO TID
- **Patient:** 22 lb child
- **Safe dose:** 20-40 mg/kg/day in 3 divided doses
- **Convert weight:** 22 lb / 2.2 = 10 kg
- **Calculate safe range:**
  - Low: 10 kg × 20 mg = 200 mg/day
  - High: 10 kg × 40 mg = 400 mg/day
- **Ordered daily dose:** 250 mg × 3 = 750 mg/day
- **Evaluation:** **NOT SAFE** - exceeds maximum safe dose

#### Example 2: Acetaminophen
- **Ordered:** 160 mg PO q4h PRN
- **Patient:** 15 kg child
- **Safe dose:** 10-15 mg/kg/dose, max 5 doses/24hr
- **Calculate safe range:**
  - Low: 15 kg × 10 mg = 150 mg/dose
  - High: 15 kg × 15 mg = 225 mg/dose
- **Evaluation:** **SAFE** - 160 mg is within 150-225 mg range

### Body Surface Area (BSA) - Pediatric Dosing

**Formula (most common):**
```
√(Height (cm) × Weight (kg) / 3,600) = BSA (m²)
```

**OR**

```
√(Height (inches) × Weight (lb) / 3,131) = BSA (m²)
```

**For dosing:**
```
Ordered dose (per m²) × Patient's BSA (m²) = Dose to give
```

#### Example:
- **Ordered:** Chemotherapy 50 mg/m²
- **Patient:** Height 100 cm, Weight 20 kg
- **Calculate BSA:** √(100 × 20 / 3,600) = √0.556 = **0.75 m²**
- **Calculate dose:** 50 mg/m² × 0.75 m² = **37.5 mg**

## Intake & Output Calculations

### Fluid Balance

**Formula:**
```
Total Intake - Total Output = Fluid Balance
```

**Positive balance** = More intake than output (fluid retention)
**Negative balance** = More output than intake (dehydration)

#### Example:
**Intake (8-hour shift):**
- IV fluids: 800 mL
- PO fluids: 240 mL (8 oz)
- Ice chips: 120 mL (melted = half of amount)
- **Total intake:** 1,160 mL

**Output:**
- Urine: 900 mL
- Emesis: 100 mL
- **Total output:** 1,000 mL

**Fluid Balance:** 1,160 - 1,000 = **+160 mL** (positive balance)

### Insensible Fluid Loss
- **Normal:** 500-1,000 mL/day
- **Increased with:** Fever, sweating, hyperventilation, burns

## Special Calculations

### Creatinine Clearance (Kidney Function)

**Cockcroft-Gault Formula (Male):**
```
(140 - Age) × Weight (kg)
───────────────────────── = CrCl (mL/min)
72 × Serum Creatinine
```

**For females:** Multiply result × 0.85

#### Example:
- **Patient:** 70-year-old male, 80 kg, Creatinine 1.5 mg/dL
- **Calculate:** (140 - 70) × 80 / (72 × 1.5) = 5,600 / 108 = **51.8 mL/min**
- **Interpretation:** Normal is >60 mL/min; This indicates decreased renal function

### IV Drip Rate Adjustments

**To calculate remaining time:**
```
Volume remaining (mL)
──────────────────── = Hours remaining
Current rate (mL/hr)
```

**To recalculate rate if behind/ahead:**
```
Volume remaining (mL)
───────────────────── = New rate (mL/hr)
Time remaining (hr)
```

#### Example:
- **Original order:** 1,000 mL over 10 hours (100 mL/hr)
- **After 4 hours:** 500 mL infused (should have infused 400 mL)
- **Volume remaining:** 500 mL
- **Time remaining:** 6 hours
- **Recalculate rate:** 500 mL / 6 hr = **83 mL/hr**

**Note:** Check facility policy - some require new order if >25% change in rate

### Continuous Bladder Irrigation (CBI)

**Formula:**
```
Total Output - Irrigant Instilled = True Urine Output
```

#### Example:
- **Irrigant instilled:** 3,000 mL
- **Total drainage bag:** 3,500 mL
- **True urine output:** 3,500 - 3,000 = **500 mL**

## Critical Formulas Quick Reference

### Medication Administration
| Calculation | Formula |
|-------------|---------|
| **Basic dosage** | (Desired / Have) × Quantity |
| **IV rate (pump)** | Total Volume / Hours |
| **IV drip rate** | (mL/hr × Drop factor) / 60 |
| **mcg/min to mL/hr** | (mcg/min × 60) / Concentration |
| **mcg/kg/min** | (mcg/kg/min × kg × 60) / Concentration |

### Conversions
| Conversion | Formula |
|------------|---------|
| **lb to kg** | lb / 2.2 |
| **kg to lb** | kg × 2.2 |
| **F to C** | (°F - 32) × 5/9 |
| **C to F** | (°C × 9/5) + 32 |
| **mg to g** | mg / 1,000 |
| **g to mg** | g × 1,000 |
| **mcg to mg** | mcg / 1,000 |
| **mg to mcg** | mg × 1,000 |

## Rounding Rules

### General Rules:
- **Tablets:** Round to nearest half or whole tablet
- **Liquids:** Round to nearest tenth (0.1 mL)
- **IV rates:** Round to nearest whole number (mL/hr or gtts/min)
- **Pediatric doses:** May need more precision - follow facility policy
- **Insulin:** Round to nearest whole unit (no half units unless special syringe)

### When in Doubt:
- Always round to provide LESS medication if between two amounts
- Never give more than ordered
- Verify unusual doses with pharmacist or provider

## Tips for Success

### Double-Check These:
1. **Decimal point placement** - Most common error
2. **Units match** (mg vs g, mcg vs mg)
3. **Weight in kg** (not lb)
4. **Time in hours** (convert minutes to hours if needed)
5. **Drop factor** (10, 15, 20, or 60)

### Red Flags:
- Answer requires >3 tablets or capsules
- IV rate >250 mL/hr (check order)
- Pediatric dose seems too high (verify safe dose)
- Injectable volume >3 mL IM (usually max is 3 mL)
- SubQ volume >2 mL (usually max is 1-1.5 mL)

### Calculator Tips:
- Write out the problem
- Use parentheses to ensure correct order of operations
- Double-check decimal placement
- Verify answer makes sense

**Remember:** If it doesn't look right, CHECK AGAIN. Better safe than sorry!
