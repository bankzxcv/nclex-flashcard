| **Elderly** | 60-100 | 70-80 | <60 | >100 |

### Assessment Points
- **Site**: Radial (most common), apical (most accurate), brachial, carotid, femoral, pedal
- **Apical Pulse**: Count for 60 seconds; Required before digoxin, beta blockers
- **Quality**: Strong, weak, thready, bounding
- **Rhythm**: Regular, regularly irregular, irregularly irregular

### Abnormal Patterns
| Term | Definition | Rate |
|------|------------|------|
| **Tachypnea** | Rapid breathing | >20 (adult) |
| **Bradypnea** | Slow breathing | <12 (adult) |
| **Apnea** | Absence of breathing | 0 for >15 sec |
| **Dyspnea** | Difficulty breathing | N/A |
| **Orthopnea** | SOB when lying flat | N/A |

### Breathing Patterns
- **Eupnea**: Normal, regular breathing
- **Cheyne-Stokes**: Gradual increase then decrease with apnea periods
- **Kussmaul**: Deep, rapid, labored (DKA, metabolic acidosis)
- **Biot's**: Irregular with apnea periods (increased ICP)
- **Apneustic**: Prolonged inspiratory phase

### Adult Blood Pressure Categories (AHA)
| Category | Systolic (mmHg) | Diastolic (mmHg) |
|----------|----------------|------------------|
| **Normal** | <120 | AND <80 |
| **Elevated** | 120-129 | AND <80 |
| **Hypertension Stage 1** | 130-139 | OR 80-89 |
| **Hypertension Stage 2** | ≥140 | OR ≥90 |
| **Hypertensive Crisis** | >180 | AND/OR >120 |
| **Hypotension** | <90 | OR <60 |

### BP Assessment
- **Cuff Size**: 2/3 of upper arm; Wrong size = inaccurate reading
- **Position**: Seated, arm at heart level, feet flat
- **Wait Time**: Rest 5 minutes before measuring
- **Orthostatic Hypotension**: Drop of ≥20 mmHg systolic or ≥10 mmHg diastolic when standing

### Special Populations
| Condition | BP Target |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Diabetes** | <130/80 |
| **Chronic Kidney Disease** | <130/80 |
| **Age >60 (no comorbidities)** | <150/90 |
| **Pregnancy** | <140/90 |
| **Preeclampsia** | ≥140/90 (severe ≥160/110) |

## Temperature Detail

### Normal Temperature Ranges by Route
| Route | Normal Range °F | Normal Range °C | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Oral** | 97.6-99.6 | 36.4-37.5 | Most common; Wait 15 min after eating/drinking |
| **Rectal** | 98.6-100.6 | 37-38.1 | Most accurate core temp; 1°F higher than oral |
| **Axillary** | 96.6-98.6 | 35.9-37 | Least accurate; 1°F lower than oral |
| **Tympanic** | 97.2-100 | 36.2-37.8 | Quick, convenient; Pull ear up and back (adult) |
| **Temporal** | 97.2-100 | 36.2-37.8 | Non-invasive, accurate |

### Temperature Classifications
| Classification | °F | °C | Interpretation |
|----------------|----|----|----------------|
| **Hypothermia** | <95 | <35 | Medical emergency |
| **Low Normal** | 96-97 | 35.5-36 | Monitor |
| **Normal** | 97.6-99.6 | 36.4-37.5 | Normal range |
| **Low-grade Fever** | 99.6-100.4 | 37.5-38 | Mild elevation |
| **Fever (Pyrexia)** | 100.4-103 | 38-39.4 | Moderate fever |
| **High Fever** | 103-105 | 39.4-40.5 | Significant fever |
| **Hyperpyrexia** | >105.8 | >41 | Medical emergency |

### Fever Response by Age
| Age | Low-grade | Fever | High Fever |
|-----|-----------|-------|------------|
| **Infant 0-3 months** | N/A | >100.4°F (38°C) | Call provider immediately |
| **Infant 3-6 months** | 100.4-102 | 102-103 | >103°F |
| **Child >6 months** | 100.4-102 | 102-104 | >104°F |
| **Adult** | 99.6-100.4 | 100.4-103 | >103°F |

### Temperature Conversion
- **F to C**: (°F - 32) × 5/9 = °C
- **C to F**: (°C × 9/5) + 32 = °F
- **Quick estimate**: Each 1°C = 1.8°F

## Oxygen Saturation (SpO2)

| Category | SpO2 Range | Interpretation |
|----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Normal** | 95-100% | Adequate oxygenation |
| **Mild Hypoxemia** | 90-94% | Supplemental O2 may be needed |
| **Moderate Hypoxemia** | 85-89% | Supplemental O2 required |
| **Severe Hypoxemia** | <85% | Medical emergency |
| **COPD patients** | 88-92% | Acceptable range (avoid >92%) |

### SpO2 Assessment
- **Location**: Finger, toe, earlobe
- **Accuracy**: Affected by nail polish, poor circulation, carbon monoxide poisoning
- **Normal PaO2**: 80-100 mmHg (correlates with SpO2 95-100%)

## Pain Assessment (5th Vital Sign)

### Pain Scales
| Scale | Use | Range |
|-------|-----|-------|
| **Numeric (0-10)** | Adults, children >8 | 0 = no pain, 10 = worst pain |
| **FLACC** | Infants, children <3 | 0-10 based on Face, Legs, Activity, Cry, Consolability |
| **Wong-Baker FACES** | Children 3-8 years | 0-10 with face drawings |
| **CPOT** | Critically ill, intubated | 0-8 based on facial expression, body movements, ventilator compliance |
| **PAINAD** | Dementia patients | 0-10 based on breathing, vocalization, facial expression, body language, consolability |

### Pain Severity
- **Mild**: 1-3
- **Moderate**: 4-6
- **Severe**: 7-10

## Critical Values - Quick Reference

### Immediately Report If:
- **HR**: <50 or >120 bpm (adult); <100 or >180 (newborn)
- **RR**: <10 or >30 breaths/min (adult); <30 or >70 (infant)
- **BP**: SBP <90 or >180; DBP <60 or >120 (adult)
- **Temp**: <95°F or >105°F (adult); >100.4°F (infant <3 months)
- **SpO2**: <90% (or <88% in COPD)
- **Pain**: Unrelieved severe pain (7-10) despite interventions

## Special Considerations

### Pregnancy - Normal Changes
| Vital Sign | Change |
|------------|--------|
| **HR** | Increase 10-20 bpm |
| **RR** | Slight increase |
| **BP** | Decrease 5-10 mmHg in 2nd trimester |
| **Temp** | Slight increase 0.5-1°F |

### Exercise/Activity
| Vital Sign | Change |
|------------|--------|
| **HR** | Can increase to 220 - age |
| **RR** | Increases with exertion |
| **BP** | SBP increases; DBP stays same/decreases |
| **Temp** | Increases up to 103°F |

### Factors Affecting Vital Signs
- **Increase**: Pain, anxiety, fever, hypoxia, hypovolemia, medications (stimulants)
- **Decrease**: Sleep, medications (sedatives, beta blockers), hypothermia, shock (late)
- **Age**: Generally decrease with age (except BP)
- **Time of Day**: Lowest in early morning, highest in late afternoon/evening

## Pediatric Assessment Tips

### Measurement Order
1. **Respirations** - Count first while child is quiet
2. **Heart Rate** - Auscultate apically for 60 seconds
3. **Temperature** - Non-invasive routes first
4. **Blood Pressure** - Last (often upsets child)

### Site Selection by Age
